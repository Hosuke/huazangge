"The Doctrine of the Mean (中庸) is one of the core texts of the Confucian canon, forming part of the Four Books compiled by Zhu Xi in the Song dynasty. Traditionally attributed to Zisi (孔子之孫), the text consists of 33 chapters that explore the concept of the 'mean' (中), defined as the balanced path that avoids extremes. The work begins with the famous statement: 'The master said: The mean is the highest virtue' (子曰：「中庸之為德也，其至矣乎!」). The core teaching is that moral equilibrium arises when the emotions (喜怒哀樂) are kept in a state of 'central harmony' (中和). When the mind is neutral before the arousal of emotions, the individual can act in accordance with the Way (道) and achieve harmony with the cosmos.
