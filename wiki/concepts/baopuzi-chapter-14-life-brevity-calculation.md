## English

This passage from Volume 14 of Ge Hong's Inner Chapters presents one of the most striking calculations of human lifespan in classical Daoist literature. Ge Hong opens with an extended metaphor comparing human life to livestock being led to the slaughterhouse, observing that "as one advances one step, one draws closer to death" (每進一步，而去死轉近).

The calculation proceeds with meticulous specificity: the nominal human lifespan of one hundred years (百年之壽) translates to merely thirty thousand days (三萬餘日). This gross figure, however, undergoes substantial reduction upon closer analysis. Childhood renders one incapable of knowledge; old age brings an end to joy; the periods of youth and senility consume several decades, while illness and hardship (險隘憂病) perpetually attend one's journey through existence.

Ge Hong's arithmetic is precise: even for those fortunate enough to reach one hundred years, if one experiences continuous joy and peace (喜笑平和), the actual period of meaningful life extends to no more than fifty or sixty years. For most, "哀憂昏耄，六七千日耳，顧眄已盡矣"—a mere six or seven thousand days before one turns to look back and finds everything already exhausted. "Those who truly reach one hundred years are perhaps one in ten thousand."

The passage draws a pointed comparison with the summer insect (夏蟲) and the morning mushroom (朝菌), creatures whose brief lifespans are traditionally invoked to illustrate the transience of existence. Ge Hong suggests that those who do not understand the Way (不知道者) face a particularly tragic situation, for they exhaust their finite days in pursuit of wealth, power, and sensual pleasure without recognizing the urgency of cultivating the means of transcendence.

The concluding philosophical reflection observes that "those who understand the Way do not worry about death—not because they do not desire life, but because they genuinely do not know the methods by which death might be avoided, and so they simply engage in empty anxious worry, which benefits nothing." This results in the attitude of "樂天知命，故不憂" (rejoicing in Heaven and understanding one's fate, hence without worry), which Ge Hong cites approvingly, distinguishing it from a mere absence of desire for longevity.

## 中文

抱朴子卷十四對人生壽命之計算，為古典道教文學中最觸目驚心者之一。葛洪以牽牛羊赴屠所為喻，感嘆「每進一步，而去死轉近」。彼进而進行精密計算：百年之壽，折合三萬餘日。然此總數尚需大加折扣：幼弱則未有所知，衰邁則歡樂並廢，童蒙昏耄，除數十年，而險隘憂病，相尋代有。

其算術精確：即得百年之壽，若喜笑平和，不過五、六十年；至於「咄嗟滅盡，哀憂昏耄，六七千日耳，顧眄已盡矣」。況於全百年者，萬未有一乎？此與夏蟲朝菌之生命短暫相較，亦無以笑彼。

葛洪感歎「不知道者之所至悲矣」——彼不知修仙之道，徒以勢利嗜欲耗費光陰，空委二親之供奉，捐妻子而不卹，終無所成。本文又辨「樂天知命，故不憂」之義：達人非不欲久生，乃不知所以免死之術，故只好安時處順，順其自然。

## 日本語

抱朴子第十四巻における人間の寿命の計算は、古典道教文学の中で最も衝撃的なものとして位置づけられる。葛洪は牛羊を屠殺場に連れて行くことに例え、「一歩進むごとに死に近づく」と歎じている。

精密な計算によれば、百年という名目上の寿命は実際には三万余日に過ぎない。しかし Childhoodは知識を獲得できず、老年では喜びも終わってしまう。若者と老人の時期は数十年間続き、病苦と困難が常に付きまとう。幸運にも百年生存したとしても、真正の喜びと平和を楽しめるのは五六十年程度であり、実際には六七千日程度ですら得られない。

「全百年を全うする者は一万人に一人もいない」という指摘は、死を避ける方法を知らない者の悲劇的な状況を描いている。葛洪は、真の悟りを開いた者は死を恐れないとしながらも、それは単に方法がわからないからであり、天命を喜んで受け入れるという「楽しみと運命を知る」という姿勢こそが重要であると主張している。
