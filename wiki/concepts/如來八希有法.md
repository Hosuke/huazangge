## English

The “Eight Rare Teachings of the Tathāgata” (如來八希有法) is a short doctrinal list found in the Mahāyāna sūtra *The Sūtra of the Great Firm Brahman* (佛說大堅固婆羅門緣起經). In this text the Buddha enumerates eight conditions that are exceptionally rare in the world, and stresses that the presence of these eight factors is essential for the practicer to achieve the ultimate liberation of nirvāṇa. The list serves both as a reminder of the fleeting opportunities for spiritual progress and as a guide for cultivators to cherish and preserve the favorable circumstances they encounter.

The eight rare conditions are:

1. **Encountering a Buddha’s appearance** – the rare chance to see a Buddha manifest in the world.
2. **Hearing the true Dharma** – being able to listen to the authentic teachings.
3. **Birth in a central kingdom** – being born in a region where the Dharma is actively taught.
4. **Having the five sense faculties intact** – possessing healthy senses to perceive the teachings.
5. **Maintaining pure precepts** – observing the moral precepts without violation.
6. **Practicing concentration and insight** – engaging in meditative cultivation (śamatha‑vipaśyanā).
7. **Meeting good spiritual friends** – associating with wise and compassionate teachers.
8. **Attaining non‑regression** – achieving a stage of practice where one cannot fall back.

The sutra emphasizes that each of these factors is “rare” (希有) because they seldom occur together. When a practitioner is blessed with all eight, the path to final emancipation becomes accessible, whereas their absence makes progress extremely difficult. The teaching is thus used in later Buddhist literature as a mnemonic device to inspire gratitude, diligent practice, and the compassionate dedication of merit to all sentient beings so that others may also attain these rare conditions.

「如來八希有法」出自《大堅固婆羅門緣起經》，是佛陀列舉的八種世間罕見的條件。這八項法門被認為是修行者能夠究竟解脫的必要前提，只有同時具足這些難得的機緣，才能確保走向涅槃的道路暢通無阻。

八項稀有之法如下：

1. **佛出世** – 能夠親遇佛陀降臨世間的難得機會。
