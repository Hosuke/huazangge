## English

In Baopuzi Volume 8, Ge Hong offers a critical evaluation of the Daoist textual tradition, arguing that most existing "Daoist books" are incomplete, misleading, or unsuitable for serious cultivation practice. This section represents an important contribution to the early medieval discourse on textual authority and proper methodology in religious practice.

Ge Hong observes that Daoist texts (道書) supposedly originating from Huang-Lao are actually quite few in number. Most of what circulates as Daoist literature consists of writings by later enthusiasts who expanded upon limited insights, resulting in massive collections that are fundamentally deficient. "Ancient people were plain and honest, and most lacked talent. Their discussions of physical principles were incomplete, their textual evidence was not clearly documented—all were lacking in essentials and difficult to understand, explanations not deep or thorough enough to elaborately convey subtle teachings, reveal the perplexed, encourage the aspiring, or teach beginners about the pathways of the mysterious and the sources of fortune and misfortune."

The text specifically criticizes the **Daodejing** (五千文), stating that although it issued from Laozi, it contains only general discussions and abbreviated presentations. "One cannot find complete accounts of matters, only sections that can be verified. Simply reciting this classic without understanding the essential Way is merely futile labor—and how much more so for those who haven't even reached that level?"

Zhuangzi, Guan Yinzi, and the writings attributed to Guan Lingxi and Yin Xi are similarly dismissed: "Although their literary style follows Huang-Lao and takes玄虛 (mysterious emptiness) as its model, they merely elaborate broad themes and never reach the highest teachings. Some even identify life and death as identical, claiming there is no difference between living as corvée and dying as rest—such statements are already thousands of miles from true transcendence. How could such writings be worth studying and enjoying?"

Ge Hong's conclusion is that those who merely study texts without finding genuine teachers will never achieve transcendence: "I have heard that those who seek fish by the river merely from gazing at the water will not catch any, not because the river lacks fish, but because they have no net."

The proper approach, according to Ge Hong, is to carefully select the best texts after thorough examination, to seek genuine transmission from authentic teachers, and to focus on practical cultivation rather than endless textual accumulation. This critical attitude toward textual study while emphasizing direct transmission represents a distinctive feature of early Daoist practice and would influence later developments in [[xuandao-mysterious-way]] cultivation traditions.

## 中文

葛洪在《抱朴子·內篇》卷八中對道教文獻傳統提出了尖銳的批評，認為現存的所謂「道書」大多殘缺不全、誤導讀者，或根本不適合認真的修煉實踐。此段文字對早期中古時期的文本權威與宗教修行方法論話語作出了重要貢獻。

葛洪指出，所謂出於黃老的道書數量極少，大多是後世好事者各以所見聞而增廣蔓衍，致使篇卷至於山積。古人質樸，又多無才，其所述物理不周悉，所證按不著明，皆闕所要而難解，解之又不能不深遠，不足以演暢微言，開示憤悱，勸進有志，教戒始學。

《抱朴子》特別批評了《道德經》（五千文）：「《老子》五千文雖出老子，然皆泛論較略耳。其中了不肯首尾全舉其事，有可承按者也。但暗誦此經，而不得要道，直為徒勞耳，又況不及者乎？」

至於《莊子》《關尹子》及令尹喜之徒之文，《抱朴子》評價道：「其屬文筆，雖祖述黃老，憲章玄虛，但演其大旨，永無至言。或複齊死生，謂無異以存活為徭役，以殂歿為休息，其去神仙，已千億裡矣，豈足耽玩哉？」

葛洪的結論是，僅靠讀書而不得明師者，終不能成仙：「又聞之，求仙以臨河羨魚，而無網罟，非河中之無魚也。」

正確的方法應是博覽而詳擇其善者，留意於要道，不足尋繹者則不足觀。此種對文本學習的批判態度與對直接傳承的強調，代表了早期道教實踐的顯著特徵。

## 日本語

抱朴子第八巻において、葛洪は道教文献の伝統に対して鋭い批判を展開し、現存する「道書」の多くは不完全であり、誤った導となり、あるいは真剣な修煉実践に適しないと論じた。

葛洪によれば、黃老に起源すると稱される道書は実際には非常に少なく、後世の好事家が限られた知見を拡大解釈して山ほどの文献を作り上げたものである。「古代人は質朴で才能にも欠けていた。彼らの物理に関する論述は不完全であり、文献的証拠は明確に記されていない。すべてが必要不可欠な点を欠いて理解困難であり、解説も深遠さに欠けて微言を演暢し、戸惑う者を启迪し、有志を励まし、初学者を教えるに足りない」。

『道徳経』（五千文）についても具体的に批判し、「『老子』五千文は確かに老子から出たものだが、概論的で略述的な内容に過ぎない。首尾一貫した叙述見つけられず、検証可能な箇所のみが残っている。この経を暗唱しても要道を得なければ、それは単なる無益な労力にすぎない。さらに及ばない者はなおさらである」と論じた。

『荘子』『関尹子』以及其他の著作についても、「黄老を祖述し玄虚を模範とする文体だが、根本的な教説を述べるだけで最高真理に達することはなく、死生を一如とし、生存を労役、死去を休息と同一視するものもあり、これは真の仙人からすでに千億里もかけ離れている」と批判した。
