## English

**Arhat Fruit (阿羅漢果)** is a central concept in Buddhist doctrine denoting the final result of the Arhat’s spiritual attainment. An Arhat (阿羅漢), literally “worthy one,” is a practitioner who has eradicated all defilements—greed, hatred, and delusion—thereby achieving liberation from samsāra (the cycle of birth and death). The attainment of 阿羅漢果 is described as the realization of nirvāṇa without remainder, the full comprehension of the Four Noble Truths, and the practice of the Noble Eightfold Path to its culmination.

In the early Buddhist texts, the stage of 阿羅漢果 follows the three lower fruits of the śrāvaka path: Stream‑Enterer (須陀洹果), Once‑Returner (斯陀含果), and Non‑Returner (阿那含果). The Arhat, having passed beyond these preliminary levels, is said to be completely free from the five aggregates (色、受、想、行、識) and no longer subject to future rebirth.

Scriptural accounts frequently illustrate the attainment of 阿羅漢果 with vivid narratives. One such account is the story of the monk **大堅固** (Mahākāśyapa), who, after renouncing his royal position, wives, and thousands of followers, left worldly life, attained the 阿羅漢果, and thereafter taught the Dharma, leading many to liberation in various heavenly realms.

While the Arhat ideal is most prominent in Theravāda Buddhism, Mahāyāna texts also refer to the concept, often contrasting it with the bodhisattva ideal, yet still recognizing the Arhat’s high level of spiritual realization.

## 中文

**阿羅漢果**是佛教教義中的核心概念，指的是阿羅漢修行者最終的精神成就。阿羅漢，意為「值得尊敬者」，是指已彻底断除貪、瞋、癡三毒，從而脫離生死輪迴、獲得解脫的修行人。獲得阿羅漢果被描述為證得無餘涅槃、圓滿四聖諦、通達八正道並最終達到究竟安穩的境界。

在早期佛教經典中，阿羅漢果位於聲聞乘的三果之上：須陀洹果（預流果）、斯陀含果（一來果）、阿那含果（不還果）。達到阿羅漢果的修行者已超越五蘊，不復受未來生死之苦。

經文中常以生動的故事說明阿羅漢果的證得，如大堅固（摩訶迦葉）的事例：大堅固拋棄王位、妻子及萬千弟子，剃度出家，最終證得阿羅漢果，宣說佛法，帶領無數眾生往生各層天界，得以解脫。

雖然阿羅漢理想在聲聞佛教（上部座）中最为突出，大乘經典亦提及此概念，常將其與菩薩理想作對比，但仍肯定阿羅漢果的高層次精神成就。
