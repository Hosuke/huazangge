## English

The Five Roots (五根) and Five Powers (五力) represent two complementary sets of spiritual faculties within the framework of the [[thirty-seven-factors-enlightenment|三十七品]] (Thirty-Seven Factors of Enlightenment). The Han Dynasty commentary on the Mindfulness Sutra provides an early Chinese exposition of these doctrines, explaining their nature, functions, and interrelationships.

### The Five Roots

The Five Roots are defined as:

1. **Faith (信根, *śraddhā*)** — The root of trust and confidence in the Triple Gem and the law of karmic causation
2. **Effort (精進根, *vīrya*)** — The root of vigorous energy in practicing the Dharma
3. **Mindfulness (念根, *smṛti*)** — The root of clear and stable recollection
4. **Concentration (定根, *samādhi*)** — The root of unified and collected mental focus
5. **Wisdom (慧根, *prajñā*)** — The root of discriminative insight into the nature of reality

The commentary explains the significance of these roots through agricultural metaphor: "Just as a tree without roots cannot grow, the path without faith cannot be established. Faith establishes the root of the path, and only then does the path become established; therefore faith is primary."

### The Five Powers

The Five Powers are the same five spiritual faculties, but developed to their fullest extent:

1. **Faith Power (信力)** — Faith so strong that it cannot be shaken by worldly attractions
2. **Effort Power (精進力)** — Energy so powerful that it benefits all beings
3. **Mindfulness Power (念力)** — Recollection so stable that not even thunder can disturb the ear
4. **Concentration Power (定力)** — Samādhi so deep that the mind abides unshakeably
5. **Wisdom Power (慧力)** — Insight so clear that all dharmas are seen as empty

The commentary defines the quality of "power" (*力*) through several characteristics:

- **Imperviability (無有能得壞為力義)**: That which cannot be corrupted or destroyed
- **Beneficial Effect (有所益為力義)**: That which brings genuine benefit to beings
- **Courage (有膽為力義)**: That which enables the practitioner to confront and subdue obstacles
- **Reliance (能得依為力義)**: That which can be relied upon as an unshakable foundation

### The Transformation of Roots into Powers

The commentary explains that when the Four Foundations of Mindfulness are cultivated, the Five Roots are established. When the Five Roots are established, the Five Powers are generated. This progression represents the maturation of spiritual faculties from nascent potential to full realization.

The text states: "Those who have attained the Four Foundations of Mindfulness have the Five Roots immediately established; when the Five Roots are established, the path-power (道力) is accomplished. Even the most beautiful heavenly maidens cannot disturb their vision, the sweetest music cannot damage their hearing, the choicest perfumes and delicacies cannot entice them, even the honor of Mara or an emperor cannot mislead them. Their six senses are not confused, their mind is like empty space, nothing can move them—this is called power."

### Role in Liberation

The Five Roots and Powers serve as the mediating factors between the preliminary practices (such as the Four Foundations and Four Right Exertions) and the higher stages of the path (including the Seven Factors of Awakening and the Noble Eightfold Path). They represent the consolidation of spiritual practice into stable, reliable faculties that support the development of wisdom and the realization of nirvana.

## 中文

五根五力為三十七品之重要法門，反映佛教對心靈修養力量之系統分析。東漢安世高譯注之《陰持入經》對此有詳盡闡釋。

### 五根

五根者：

1. **信根** — 對三寶及業因果報之信仰與信心
2. **精進根** — 修行佛法之勇猛精力
3. **念根** — 清明穩定之記憶與覺知
4. **定根** — 專一集中之心靈專注
5. **慧根** — 對諸法實相之辨別智慧

《陰持入經》以樹根為喻：「樹非根不生、道非信不成，為道德之根，信根立、道乃成，故信為首。」此明信根為五根之首，乃入道之基。

### 五力

五力者，五根之力量增長至究竟者也：

1. **信力** — 不為天女玉色所亂其目
2. **精進力** — 德潤群生之益
3. **念力** — 魔王億八千萬兵不能動菩薩一毛
4. **定力** — 雷震之響不能聞其耳
5. **慧力** — 了一切法空

### 根力之關係

《陰持入經》云：「夫已得四意止者五根即立，五根立道力即成。」修行者由四意止得五根，由五根得五力，此乃心靈修養之必然進程。五力已成，則解脫可期。

## 日本語

五根五力（ごこんごりょく）は{{wp:三十七道品|三十七品}}における重要な法門である。漢代の安世高の註釈において詳しく説明されており、精神的能力の体系的な分析を提示している。

### 五根

五根とは：
1. **信根（*{{wp:śraddhā}}*)** — 三宝と因果応報への信仰と信頼
2. **精進根（*{{wp:vīrya}}*)** — 法を実践するための精力
3. **念根（*{{wp:smṛti}}*)** — 明瞭で安定した記憶と気づき
4. **定根（*{{wp:samādhi}}*)** — 統一された精神の集中
5. **慧根（*{{wp:prajñā}}*)** — 実在の本性を見分ける知恵

註釈は木と根の喩えを用いて「木は根がなければ生えず、道は信がなければ建立されない」と述べる。

### 五力

五力とは同じ五つの精神的能力が完全に発達したものである：

1. **信力** — 最も美しい天女も視線を乱せないほどの信仰
2. **精進力** — すべての生けるものを益するエネルギー
3. **念力** — 雷鳴さえ耳を驚かせないほどの気づきの安定性
4. **定力** — 心を揺るがすことのない深みのある瞑想
5. **慧力** — すべての法を空として見る明晰な洞察
