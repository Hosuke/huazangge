## English

### Overview
Mencius (Mengzi, 372–289  BCE) is the most prominent Confucian philosopher after Confucius. His political thought, recorded in the *Mencius* (《孟子》), expands on the Confucian ideal of a benevolent ruler and the moral foundations of statecraft.

### Core Principles
1. **Benevolent Governance (Ren‑Zheng)** – The ruler must embody *ren* (benevolence). A government that cares for the welfare of the people is legitimate; one that exploits them forfeits its right to rule.
2. **Mandate of Heaven (Tian Ming)** – The “Mandate of Heaven” is not hereditary but conditional on the ruler’s virtue. If a sovereign fails to govern morally, Heaven withdraws its mandate, allowing the rise of a new, virtuous ruler.
3. **People as the Foundation (Min Ben)** – Mencius famously argued that the welfare of the people outweighs the authority of the sovereign: “The people are the most important, the state is secondary, the ruler is the lightest.”
4. **Innate Goodness & Moral Cultivation** – All humans possess the “four beginnings” (four seeds of virtue). A ruler must cultivate his own moral nature to set an example, thereby inspiring the populace.
5. **Righteous Criticism** – Officials have the duty to remonstrate with unjust rulers. Persistent tyranny can be resisted, even by force, if it endangers the people.

### Political Implications
- **Critique of Despotism**: Mencius condemned rulers who pursued personal gain over public good, calling them “tyrants” and suggesting that the people have a right to replace them.
- **Ideal of the “Sage‑King”**: The perfect ruler is a “sagely monarch” whose moral virtue aligns with the cosmic order, leading to social harmony.
- **Role of the Literati (Shi)**: Scholars and officials serve as moral guides, advising rulers and ensuring that policies reflect Confucian values.

### Influence and Legacy
Mencius’s ideas became a cornerstone of later Confucian political theory, influencing:
- **Han Dynasty** state ideology and the concept of “humaneness” in governance.
- **Song‑Ming Neo‑Confucianism**, especially the emphasis on moral self‑cultivation.
- **Modern Chinese political thought**, where his emphasis on the people’s primacy informs debates on democracy and authoritarianism.

## 中文

### 概述
孟子（约公元前372—前289），名轲，孔子后最重要的儒家学者之一。其政治哲学记载于《孟子》一书中，核心在于仁政、民本与天命的关系。

### 核心原则
1. **仁政（Ren‑Zheng）**：君主必须以仁爱之心治理国家，若只顾私利则失去统治合法性。
2. **天命（Mandate of Heaven）**：天命的授予取决于君主的德行，若君主失德，天命可被收回，转授予有德者。
3. **民本（People‑Oriented）**：孟子强调“民为贵，社稷次之，君为轻”，即百姓福祉是政权的根本。
4. **人性善与道德修养**：人天生具备“四端”（仁、义、礼、智的萌芽），君主必须先修身养性，以身作则。
5. **正义批评与抵抗**：臣子有义务直言规劝暴君；若君主持续残暴，民众有权推翻其统治。

### 政治意义
- **批判专制**：孟子对不顾百姓福祉的统治者提出严厉批评，主张以民意为依归的政治合法性。
- **圣王理想**：理想的君主是以德服人的“圣王”，其统治与天地秩序相合，带来天下太平。
- **士的角色**：知识分子（士）承担教化君主、监督政策的职责，是实现仁政的重要力量。
