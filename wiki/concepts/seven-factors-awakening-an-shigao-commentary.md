## English

The Seven Factors of Awakening (七覺意, *sapta-bojjhaṅgā*), also known as the Seven Limbs of Enlightenment, constitute one of the most important groupings within the [[thirty-seven-factors-enlightenment|三十七品]] (Thirty-Seven Factors of Enlightenment). The Han Dynasty commentary on the Mindfulness Sutra provides an early Chinese exposition of these factors, detailing their individual characteristics and their role in the development of awakening.

### The Seven Factors Defined

1. **Mindfulness (念覺意, *sati*)** — The factor that remembers and maintains awareness of the teaching
2. **Discrimination of Dharma (法分別觀覺意, *dhamma-vicaya*)** — The factor that analyzes and discriminates between wholesome and unwholesome states
3. **Effort (精進覺意, *vīrya*)** — The factor of vigorous energy applied to the practice
4. **Joy (喜覺意, *pīti*)** — The factor of delight and enthusiasm in the Dharma
5. **Tranquility (猗覺意, *passaddhi*)** — The factor of calmness and ease of body and mind
6. **Concentration (定覺意, *samādhi*) — The factor of collected and unified mental focus**
7. **Equanimity (捨覺意, *upekkhā*)** — The factor of balanced equanimity toward all phenomena

### Detailed Exposition

#### Mindfulness (念覺意)

The commentary states: "Awareness is awareness of what is wholesome and what is unwholesome. When unwholesome thoughts arise, they should be destroyed; when wholesome thoughts of the path arise, they should be preserved. This is called the mindfulness factor of awakening."

#### Discrimination of Dharma (法分別觀覺意)

"Discrimination is the contemplation of wholesome and unwholesome dharmas. Through tranquil and truthful observation, one discriminates the genuine from the false, selects the pure dharma, and thereby can escape the three realms. The Anapanasutra speaks of this as 'selecting the dharma factor of awakening.'"

#### Effort (精進覺意)

While effort as a factor of awakening shares the same nature as the effort discussed in the Five Powers, here it is characterized by the joy and enthusiasm that accompany the progress toward awakening.

#### Joy (喜覺意)

The commentary notes: "The foolish delight in the six unwholesome objects, considering them precious. The wise contemplating the twelve divine spirits recognize their impermanent nature, transform the mind, and receive the superior teaching of the three factors of awakening. The ten-part teaching says: 'Use upright effort to eliminate unwholesome attachments.'"

#### Tranquility (猗覺意)

"The wise always rest their minds on the Four Foundations of Mindfulness. When the four aggregates of feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness arise, they immediately recognize and extinguish them. This is called the tranquility factor of awakening."

#### Concentration (定覺意)

"Having obtained the Four Right Exertions, the Four Bases of Supernatural Power, the Five Roots, and the Five Powers, the mind immediately becomes calm and concentrated. One abides in one's chosen meditation object, discriminates and removes impurities, guards the pure path, and investigates its ultimate meaning. With virtuous mind advancing toward the path, even thunder's roar cannot reach the ear—this is called concentration."

#### Equanimity (護覺意)

"Mind is difficult to guard and hard to master. If the mind is in the realm of desire, guard it carefully and bring it forth from desire; if it is in the realm of form or formlessness, guard it likewise. This is called the equanimity factor of awakening."

### Integration with the Path

The Seven Factors of Awakening represent the maturation of the spiritual faculties from the preliminary stages through the development of concentration and into the higher insight of wisdom. The commentary explicitly states that the Buddha, through these seven factors of purified samādhi, attains inexhaustible wisdom. The text quotes the [[human-immortal-sutra-ren-xian-jing|Human Immortal Sutra]]: "The Buddha alone among the three ones is honored, able to teach the seven factors of awakening, for the indolent, the undegenerate dharma—this samādhi is inexhaustible."

## 中文

七覺意，又稱七菩提分、七覺支，為三十七品之一，乃趨向覺悟之七種要素。東漢安世高譯注之《陰持入經》對此有詳盡闡釋。

### 七覺意

1. **念覺意** — 覺知善惡，惡念生即滅之，道念生即攝持
2. **法分別觀覺意** — 觀善惡法，寂諦觀，分別真偽、擇取淨法
3. **精進覺意** — 勇猛精進，趣向覺悟
4. **喜覺意** — 於法生喜，以道法為樂
5. **猗覺意** — 身心輕安，得止觀之樂
6. **定覺意** — 心住一境，不為外境所動
7. **護覺意** — 守護心念，不令邪念侵入

### 各覺意詳解

《陰持入經》詳述七覺意之意義及修習方法，強調此七法為佛陀所說三十七品之核心，乃三世諸佛共通之教法。
