## English

The **Five Aggregates Contemplation** (五陰觀, also 五蘊觀 or 五盛陰觀) is a systematic meditative practice derived from the Buddhist teachings on the five aggregates (skandhas) that constitute sentient experience. In the *Seven Places and Three Contemplations Sutra* (七處三觀經), this practice is presented as part of the "Seven Places and Three Contemplations" framework, representing one of the most comprehensive approaches to understanding the nature of phenomena and achieving liberation.

The five aggregates are:

1. **Form** (色, rūpa) – Physical matter, including the body and sensory objects. In the sutra, contemplation on form involves recognizing it as composed of the four great elements (earth, water, fire, air) and their transformations, characterized by impermanence and impurity.

2. **Sensation** (痛癢/受, vedanā) – Feeling tones arising from sensory contact: pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral sensations. The sutra teaches contemplation on how sensations arise from sensory "contact points" (栽) and are fundamentally marked by impermanence and suffering.

3. **Perception/Thought** (思想/想, sañjñā) – The mental activity of recognizing and naming objects. The sutra describes six types of perceptions corresponding to the six sensory gates.

4. **Formations/Mental Formations** (行, saṃskāra) – Volitional formations, impulses, and karma-generating volitions. This aggregate encompasses all karmically active mental factors beyond sensation and perception.

5. **Consciousness** (識, vijñāna) – Sensory and discriminative consciousness. The sutra discusses consciousness arising at the six sensory gates and its role in perpetuating the cycle of birth and death.

The *Seven Places* method examines each aggregate through seven aspects:
- **As they truly are** (如諦知) – Correct understanding of their nature
- **Origin/Conditioning** (習) – Understanding what gives rise to them
- **Cessation** (盡) – Understanding their cessation
- **The path** (道) – The eightfold noble path leading to their transcendence
- **Taste/Attachment** (味) – The pleasurable aspect that binds beings
- **Suffering** (苦) – The unsatisfactory nature that characterizes them
- **The essential/Escape** (要) – Understanding the way to liberation

Through this thorough examination, practitioners come to understand **non-self** (無我/非身), recognizing that none of the aggregates constitute an enduring, independent self. This insight is essential for the attainment of stream-entry and further stages of enlightenment.

## 中文

**五陰觀**（亦稱五蘊觀、五盛陰觀）為佛教基於五陰（色、受、想、行、識）之教理所開展之系統禪觀修行法門。《七處三觀經》以「七處三觀」之框架闡述此觀法，為理解諸法實相、趣向解脫之重要途徑。

五陰之義涵如下：

一、**色**——物質及其形態，包括內外色法，由四大（地、水、火、風）和合而成，具無常、不淨之性。

二、**受**（痛癢）——領納心想，由六根對六塵而生苦、樂、捨三受。經中詳述六觸處（栽）生六受，皆無常、苦、空。

三、**想**（思想）——取像認知之心所，由六根攀緣六塵而生種種名言、分別。

四、**行**——遷流造作之心所，包括思心所及種種相應心所，能起善惡業，輪轉生死。

五、**識**——了別認識之心王，於六根門緣六塵而了別，為生死輪迴之主體。

**七處**之意，乃對每一陰皆以七種角度觀察：
- 如諦知（正確認知）
- 習（生起因緣）
- 盡（滅盡證滅）
- 道（八正道之修習）
- 味（染著之味）
- 苦（苦惱之本質）
- 要（出離之要法）

透過此全面觀照，行者得以通達**無我**之理，認識五陰皆無常、無我、無自性，由此斷除我見、我執，趣向解脫。此為聖道之根本階梯，大小乘共許之修行要門。

## 日本語

**五蘊観想**（ごうんかんそう/五陰の瞑想）は、衆生の経験を構成する五つの蘊（ごん/集積物）に関する仏教の教えから派生した体系的な瞑想実践である。『七処三観経』において、この実践は「七処と三观」の枠組みの一部として提示され、現象の性質を理解し解脱を達成するための最も包括的なアプローチの一つを表している。

五つの蘊とは：

1. **色**（しき/物質）——肉体と感覚対象を含む物理的な物質。経典では、色の観想は四大元素（地、水、火、風）から構成され、無常と不浄を特徴とすることを認識することを含む。

2. **受**（じゅ/感覚）——感覚的接触から生じる感覚：快、不快、中性の感覚。経典は、感覚が感覚の「接触点」（栽）から生起し、本質的に無常と苦によって特徴付けられることを教导している。

3. **想**（そう/知覚/思考）—— объектовを認識し名付ける精神的活動。経典は六つの感覚門に対応する六種類の知覚を説明している。

4. **行**（ぎょう/形成/精神的形成）——意志形成、衝動、カármic 生成意志。この蘊は感覚と知覚を超えたすべての karma的にActiveな精神的要因包含する。

5. **識**（しき/意識）——感覚的・識別的意識。六つの感覚門で生起し、生死のサイクルを持続させる役割について経典で議論されている。

**七処**の方法は、七つの側面から各蘊を調べる：
- **如其性**（如諦知）——その性質の正しい理解
- **起源/条件付け**（習）——何が生み出すかの理解
- **止滅**（盡）——その止滅の理解
- **道**（道）——それを超越する八正道
- **味/執着**（味）——衆生を縛る快楽的な側面
- **苦**（苦）——それらを特徴とする不満の性質
- **本質/脱出**（要）——解脱への道を理解すること

この徹底的な検査を通じて、実践者は**無我**（むが/非我）を理解し、五つの蘊のいずれ भीचれていない、独立した自己を構成しないことを認識する。この洞察は、須陀洹（しゅだおん/預流）とさらなる悟りの段階の達成にとって不可欠である。

## Eastern Han Exposition on Aggregate-Formation (阿含口解)

    The *Āgama Commentary* (阿含口解十二因緣經) provides an elaborate typology of the five aggregates (五陰, also written 五蘊) in relation to the twelve links of dependent origination. According to this text, the five aggregates arise from ten constituent factors (十事) of the body: killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, divisive speech, harsh speech, false speech, and idle speech (七事 related to form/色) combined with jealousy, ill will, and delusion (三事 related to the feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness aggregates). The text systematically maps each of the twelve links to specific aggregates and karmic factors, providing a detailed matrix of how saṃsāric existence is constructed through the interaction of these elements.

    A distinctive feature of this commentary is its analysis of the **three aspects of bodily existence**: (1) the aggregates in their盛陰 (盛/disordered) state, (2) the eighteen dhātus (十八種) or eighteen realms including the nineteen sense roots, and (3) the twelve links of dependent origination. The text explains that when consciousness (識) arises, it receives a body through the conditions of name-and-form (名色); the five aggregates then form the material and psychological basis for continued existence. The commentary also introduces the concept of the **ten evil actions** (十惡) as the fundamental karmic activities that generate the five aggregates and perpetuate the cycle: killing corresponds to name-and-form, stealing to the six sense fields, sexual misconduct to contact, divisive speech to feeling, false speech to craving, and so forth. This systematic correlation demonstrates how ethical misconduct directly generates the conditions for rebirth and suffering.
