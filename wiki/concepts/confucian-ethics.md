## English
The highest realm of morality is the integration of thought and action, where individuals consciously adhere to ethical norms rather than acting under compulsion. This principle is universally applicable to everyone.

### Key Concepts
1. **Filial Piety (孝)**  
   **Text**: Meng Yizi asks about filial piety. Confucius replies, “Do not violate (礼).” [...] "When they are alive, serve them with propriety; when they die, bury them with propriety and offer sacrifices with propriety."  
   **Interpretation**: Confucius emphasizes the importance of respecting familial duties according to established rituals, suggesting that true filial piety is not mere duty but an expression of respect through ritual observance.

2. **Parents' Concerns**  
   **Text**: Meng Wubo asks about filial piety. Confucius states, “Parents worry only about their children's illness.”  
   **Interpretation**: This reinforces the idea that sons should be singularly concerned about their parents' well-being, especially regarding health, establishing a deeper emotional bond.

3. **True Respect**  
   **Text**: Ziyou questions before Confucius about filial duties. Confucius states, “Nowadays, filial piety simply refers to supporting parents. However, even dogs and horses receive support. Without respect, how is this different?”  
   **Interpretation**: It critiques the contemporary understanding of filial piety, highlighting that mere financial support does not equate to genuine respect and devotion.

4. **The Attitude of the Respectful Child**  
   **Text**: Zixia inquires how to show filial piety, and Confucius replies, “It is hard to maintain a pleasant demeanor.”  
   **Interpretation**: Confucius indicates that a genuinely respectful child not only acts but does so with a positive attitude, implying a proactive stance towards family relations beyond just duties performed.

5. **Learning and Renewal**  
   **Text**: Confucius says, “Reviewing the old and learning the new is how one can become a teacher.”  
   **Interpretation**: This suggests that continuous self-reflection and education are essential for personal growth and moral advancement.

### Conclusion
Confucian ethics, particularly the concept of filial piety, involves a multifaceted approach that incorporates respect, emotional ties, and adherence to ritual, promoting holistic familial and societal harmony.

## 中文
道德的最高境界是思想和言行的融合，自觉遵守道德规范，而不是勉强而为。这一原则对任何人都是适用的。

### 核心概念
1. **孝**  
   **原文**: 孟懿子问孝，子曰：“无违。” [...] “生，事之以礼；死，葬之以礼，祭之以礼。”  
   **解读**: 孔子强调尊重家族义务的重要性，按照既定礼仪实行，真正的孝道不仅是义务，更是通过礼节的遵守表达对父母的尊敬。

2. **父母的关心**  
   **原文**: 孟武伯问孝，子曰：“父母唯其疾之忧。”  
   **解读**: 这强化了儿女特别关心父母健康的重要性，这是情感上的深层联系。

3. **真正的尊重**  
   **原文**: 子游问孝，子曰：“今之孝者，是谓能养；至于犬马，皆能有养；不敬，何以别乎？”  
   **解读**: 这一观点批判了当代对孝道的理解，指出仅仅提供经济支持不能替代真正的尊重和奉献。

4. **尊敬子女的态度**  
   **原文**: 子夏问孝，子曰：“色难。”  
   **解读**: 孔子表明，一个真正尊敬的子女不仅要行动，更要持有积极的态度，暗示家庭关系中需积极主动。

5. **学习与更新**  
   **原文**: 孔子曰：“温故而知新，可以为师矣。”  
   **解读**: 这表明持续的自我反思和学习对个人成长和道德进步至关重要。

### 结论
儒家伦理，特别是孝道的概念，涉及多面向的方式，包括尊重、情感纽带和礼仪的遵守，促进家庭和社会的整体和谐。

## 日本語

### 主要な概念
1. **孝行 (孝)**  
   **原文**: 孟懿子が孝行について尋ねると、孔子は「礼を犯してはいけない」と答えた [...] 「生きている間は礼をもって仕え、亡くなったら礼をもって葬り、礼をもって祭る。」  
   **解釈**: 孔子は、家族の義務を礼に従って尊重することの重要性を強調しています。本当の孝行は義務の遂行にとどまらず、礼の実践によって表現されるべきです。

2. **両親の関心**  
   **原文**: 孟武伯が孝行について聞き、孔子は「両親は子供の病気のことを心配する」と述べた。  
   **解釈**: これは、子供は特に両親の健康に対して一番心配しなければならないという考えを強化し、深い感情的つながりを築きます。

3. **真の尊重**  
   **原文**: 子游が孝行について尋ねると、孔子は「今の孝行は、お金を養うことだけであり、犬や馬も人に養われる。敬わなければ、どう違いがあるか」と言った。  
   **解釈**: これは、現代の理解に対する批判であり、経済的支援だけでは本当の尊敬と献身にはならないことを強調します。

4. **敬子女の態度**  
   **原文**: 子夏が孝行について尋ねると、孔子は「色難」と述べた。  
   **解釈**: 孔子は、真に敬う子供は行動するだけでなく、また肯定的な態度を持つべきです。これは、家族の関係に対してより積極的にアプローチする必要があることを示唆しています。

5. **学習と更新**  
   **原文**: 孔子は「旧知を温め新たに知ることができれば、教師にふさわしい」と述べた。  
   **解釈**: これは、自己反省と継続的な学習が個人の成長と道徳の向上に欠かせない要素であることを示しています。

### 結論
儒教の倫理、特に孝の概念は、尊重、感情的な絆、儀式の遵守を含む多面的なアプローチを持ち、家庭と社会の調和を促進します。

## English

# Confucian Ethics

Confucian ethics represents the systematic moral philosophy developed in the Confucian tradition, emphasizing virtue cultivation, social harmony, and moral responsibility through proper conduct and self-cultivation.

## Core Principles

The foundation of Confucian ethics lies in the cultivation of moral character and the practice of virtue in daily life. Central to this system is the concept of [[benevolence]] (ren), which encompasses humaneness, compassion, and the ideal relationship between individuals.

## Virtue Cultivation

Confucian ethics emphasizes continuous self-improvement through [[knowledge-learning]] and moral practice. The process involves:

- Self-reflection and [[confucian-self-examination]]
- Ritual propriety and etiquette ([[confucius-etiquette-xiang-dang]])
- Musical cultivation for moral refinement ([[confucian-music-yue]])

## Social Dimensions

The ethical framework extends to social and political realms through concepts like [[benevolent-government]] and the ideal of the [[junzi]] (superior person) who embodies moral excellence and serves as a moral exemplar in society.

## Philosophical Foundations

Confucian ethics engages with fundamental questions of [[human-nature]] and [[moral-responsibility]], providing a comprehensive system for personal and social moral development that has shaped East Asian ethical thought for millennia.

## English

# Confucian Ethics

Confucian ethics constitutes a comprehensive moral philosophy centered on relational virtues, self-cultivation, and social harmony, with profound influence on East Asian civilizations.

## Core Principles

The ethical system builds upon several foundational concepts:
- [[Benevolence]] (仁) as the supreme virtue
- Righteousness (义) as moral discernment
- Propriety (礼) as social regulation
- Wisdom (智) as practical judgment

## Developmental Framework

Confucian ethics emphasizes gradual moral development through:
- [[Confucian-self-examination|Self-examination]]
- Ritual practice and social engagement
- Literary and historical study
- [[Knowledge-learning|Integrated learning]]

## Social Dimension

Unlike individualistic ethical systems, Confucian ethics foregrounds relational obligations within the "five relationships" (ruler-minister, father-son, husband-wife, elder-younger, friend-friend), creating a socially embedded moral framework.

## Contemporary Relevance

The system continues to inform modern discussions of [[virtue-ethics]], community values, and the relationship between personal cultivation and social responsibility.

## English
Confucian ethics centers on moral self-cultivation and proper social relationships. Key concepts include:
- [[Ren]] (benevolence): The supreme virtue of humaneness
- [[Li]] (ritual propriety): Proper conduct maintaining social order
- [[Xiao]] (filial piety): Foundation of family ethics
- [[Junzi]] (exemplary person): The ideal moral character

Confucius emphasized "rectification of names" (正名) where social roles carry ethical responsibilities. The [[Analects]] document his teachings through dialogues with disciples like [[Yan Hui]] and [[Zilu]].

- 孝：家庭伦理的基础
