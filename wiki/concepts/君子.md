## English

**Junzi (君子)** is the central moral ideal in Confucian thought, denoting a person who has attained a cultivated character through self‑discipline, sincere practice of the Way (道), and the embodiment of core virtues. The term literally means “son of the lord” or “noble person,” but in the Confucian canon it refers to an individual whose conduct serves as an ethical exemplar for family, society, and governance.

### Core Virtues
- **仁 (Ren)** – benevolent concern for others  
- **义 (Yi)** – righteousness and moral duty  
- **礼 (Li)** – proper ritual and propriety  
- **智 (Zhi)** – wisdom and discernment  
- **信 (Xin)** – trustworthiness and reliability  

### Additional Qualities
- **Humility**: eliminating the “four faults” (絕四): 毋意, 毋必, 毋固, 毋我 (no arbitrary conjecture, no absolute certainty, no stubbornness, no self‑centeredness)  
- **Reverence (敬)** and **filial piety (孝)**  
- **Loyalty (忠)** and **sincere authenticity (誠)**
