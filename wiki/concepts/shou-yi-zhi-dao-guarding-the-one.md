## English

**Guarding the One** (守一, Shǒu Yī) represents one of the most fundamental and emphasized practices in Daoist alchemical and contemplative traditions. In Baopuzi Volume 18, Ge Hong dedicates extensive discussion to this practice, presenting it as the essential method for achieving immortality and spiritual efficacy.

The foundational premise, taught by his master, states: "If one knows the One, all matters are completed. One who knows the One knows nothing that is not known; one who does not know the One knows nothing that is known." This paradoxical formulation establishes the One (一, Yi) as both the origin of the Dao and the singular object of true knowledge.

### The Cosmological Significance of the One

The One (道起於一) represents the primordial emergence of the Dao, existing in supreme uniqueness ("無偶"). It manifests in three aspects—Heaven, Earth, and Humanity—to form the **Three Ones** (三一), symbolizing cosmic order. As the text explains: "Heaven obtains the One and becomes clear; Earth obtains the One and becomes peaceful; Humanity obtains the One and achieves life; Spirit obtains the One and becomes numinous."

### The Three Locations of the One

The One can be dwelling in three primary locations within the body, corresponding to the three cinnabar fields:

1. **Lower Cinnabar Field** (下丹田): Located four fen (approximately 1.2 cun) below the navel
2. **Middle Cinnabar Field** (絳宮金闕中丹田): Situated in the heart region, beneath the "crimson palace"
3. **Upper Cinnabar Field** (上丹田): Located one cun back from between the eyebrows—specifically, one cun for the Bright Hall (明堂), two cun for the Cave Chamber (洞房), and three cun for the Upper Cinnabar Field

These locations were considered sacred knowledge, transmitted only through blood oaths: "This is what Daoism values highly; generation after generation, they have sealed it with blood oaths and transmitted its names orally."

### The Practice and Its Effects

The practice of guarding the One encompasses several dimensions:

**For Hunger and Thirst**: "When thinking of the One when extremely hungry, the One provides food; when thinking of the One when extremely thirsty, the One provides drink."

**For Protection**: The One possesses personal name and attire ("姓字服色"), with the masculine form extending nine fen and the feminine six fen. One who guards the One achieves: "No sharpness can penetrate white blades; no凶 can find residence in the hundred harms."

**Against Malevolent Forces**: At ghost temples, deep forests, plague-ridden areas, tombs, tiger lairs, or serpent habitats, "guarding the One without negligence causes all malevolent forces to retreat." If one forgets and is harmed by ghosts, one should go to the central courtyard, look at the Assistant Star (輔星), grasp firmly (握固), and guard the One—the ghosts will immediately depart.

**For Avoiding Calendrical Taboos**: "One who guards the One can travel ten thousand li, enter armies, cross great rivers without needing to divine auspicious days, select times, or avoid the Year-Breaking demons, the Grand Supreme Commander, or the monthly construction taboos."

### The Practice Protocol

The transmission of the True One's oral instructions requires: a white animal sacrifice's blood, reception on a King-Xiang day (王相之日), agreements written on white silk and silver, and division of a gold contract. "Those who speak lightly and transmit recklessly will find their spirits ineffective."

### Comprehensive Benefits

The text provides an extensive list of protections and benefits:
- "On land, evil beasts are avoided; on water, dragons are repelled"
- "Demons dare not approach; blades dare not cut"
- "One who guards and does not lose can be infinite"
- "Understanding the One is not difficult; what is difficult is persisting until the end"

This practice also connects to the analogy of body-as-nation: "The chest and abdomen are like palaces; the four limbs are like border regions; the joints are like officials; the spirit is like the ruler; blood is like ministers; qi is like the people. Therefore, knowing how to govern the body enables governing the nation."

## 中文

**守一之道**為葛洪《抱朴子》內篇卷十八所述之核心修道要法，亦為道教修仙術中之根本功夫。抱朴子引其師之言：「人能知一，萬事畢。知一者，無一之不知也；不知一者，無一之能知也。」此語構成了守一說之理論基點。

### 一之起源與宇宙論意義

道起於一，其貴無偶。一之存在具有絕對唯一性，為道之顯化與萬物之本源。一分而為三，象徵天地人，是為**三一**。抱朴子云：「天得一以清，地得一以寧，人得一以生，神得一以靈。金沈羽浮，山峙川流，視之不見，聽之不聞，存之則在，忽之則亡，向之吉，背之凶。」此處之「一」既為宇宙生成之原理，亦為個體修煉之目標。

### 一之三丹田定位

守一所守者，乃是有姓字服色之真一，可居於人身三處丹田：

一者，**下丹田**：位於臍下二寸四分。
二者，**中丹田**：在心下絳宮金闕之中。
三者，**上丹田**：位於兩眉間，卻行一寸為明堂，二寸為洞房，三寸為上丹田。

此三處定位歷代口傳心授，被視為道家秘傳：「此乃是道家所重，世世歃血口傳其姓名耳。」

### 守一之功效

**延命長生**：仙經曰「子欲長生，守一當明」，飢渴之時思一即可得飲食。

**辟邪防害**：真一可護佑行者，使「白刃無所措其銳，百害無所容其凶」。在鬼廟、山林、疫地、塚墓、虎狼藪、蛇蝮處，守一不怠，眾惡遠迸。

**免除禁忌**：守一者可萬里遠行、入軍旅、涉大川，不須卜日擇時，不避太歲、月建諸神，年命之忌終不復值殃咎。

### 傳授儀軌

真一口訣之傳授有嚴格儀軌：「皆有明文，歃白牲之血，以王相之日受之，以白絹白銀為約，剋金契而分之。輕說妄傳，其神不行也。」

### 身國一如

抱朴子以身喻國：「一人之身，一國之象也。胸腹之位，猶宮室也；四肢之列，猶郊境也；骨節之分，猶百官也；神猶君也，血猶臣也，氣猶民也。故知治身，則能治國也。」此喻強調養氣全神之重要，與儒家修身治國之思想相呼應。

### 總結

守一之道兼攝宇宙論、修煉術與治國方略，為道教修仙之根本功夫。其核心在於專一守持，使行者與道合一，而達長生久視之境界。

## 日本語

**守一之道**（しゅいちしどう）は、葛洪（かっこう）の『抱朴子』内篇巻第十八において最も重視される道教修行法である。師の言葉に「大能知一、萬事畢」（「一」を知れば、萬事を成就する）とある如く、守一は道教の修仙術における根本原理として位置づけられる。

### 一の起源と宇宙論的意義

「道起於一、その貴む所偶なし」——道は「一」より生じ、その存在は絶対的な唯一性を有する。「一」は三分して天地人を象り、**三一**を形成する。『抱朴子』は言う：「天は一を得て清くなり、地は一を得て寧くなり 사람은得一以生し、神は一を得て霊となる」と。

### 一の三丹田における位置

守るべき「真一」は姓字服色を具し、人の身体内の三箇所に棲息する：

一、**下丹田**：臍下二寸四分の位置
二、**中丹田**：心下の絳宮金闕の中
三、**上丹田**：両眉間から卻行一寸の明堂、二寸の洞房、三寸の上丹田

これらの位置は道家の秘伝とし、代々血誓いで口伝されてきた。

### 守一の効用

**長生不死**：「子欲長生、守一當明」と仙経に曰く。

**邪鬼除け**：「白刃其の鋭さを措く所なく、百害其の凶を容るるなし」。

**禁忌免除**：「能守一者、行萬里、入軍旅、涉大川、不須卜日擇時」。

### 伝授の儀軌

真一口訣の伝授には厳格な儀軌がある：「白牲の血を歃い、王相の日に受け、白絹白銀を約とし、金契を剋いて分之す」。

### 身国一如の思想

『抱朴子』は身体を国家に喩える：「一人之身、一国之象也。胸腹は宮室の如く、四肢は郊境の如く、骨節は百官の如し。是の故に身を治するを知れば、国を治するを也得る」と。
