## English

**Self‑cultivation** (修身, *xiūshēn*) is the cornerstone of Confucian moral education. It denotes the disciplined effort to refine one’s character, regulate one’s emotions, and align one’s conduct with the *Dao* (the Way). In the classical Confucian canon, self‑cultivation is the first step of the great chain of social and political order: **「修身、齐家、治国、平天下」** – “cultivate the self, regulate the family, govern the state, and bring peace to the world.”

### Textual Sources
* **The Great Learning (《大学》)** – Declares that those who wish to order their families must first “rectify their minds” (正心) and “sincere their intentions” (诚意).  
* **The Doctrine of the Mean (《中庸》)** – Emphasizes *诚* (sincerity) as the ultimate virtue that arises from self‑cultivation; without *诚* nothing can be achieved.  
* **Mencius (《孟子》)** – Argues that human nature is good, and that proper cultivation can preserve and develop this innate moral sprout.  
* **Analects (《论语》)** – Highlights the importance of *慎独* (watchfulness in solitude) and continuous self‑examination.

### Core Practices
1. **Sincere Intention (诚意)** – Do not deceive oneself; let one’s likes and dislikes be genuine.  
2. **Watchfulness in Solitude (慎独)** – Monitor one’s thoughts even when no external observer is present.  
3. **Rectifying the Mind (正心)** – Remove selfish desires and emotional biases.  
4. **Investigating Things and Extending Knowledge (格物致知)** – Study the world to deepen moral insight.  
5. **Harmonizing Emotions (中和)** – Cultivate emotional equilibrium so that reactions are “neither excessive nor deficient.”

### Relationship to Other Concepts
* **Sincerity (诚)** – *修身* is the concrete practice that generates and sustains *诚*.  
* **Central Harmony (中和)** – The state of *中和* is the outcome of successful *修身*.  
* **Junzi (君子)** – The *junzi* is the person who has completed *修身* and embodies virtue in daily life.
