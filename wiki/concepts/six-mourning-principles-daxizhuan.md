## English

The Daxizhuan presents the **six principles of mourning garments and obligations** (服術有六), which form the theoretical framework for determining mourning rites, their duration, and the relationship between mourners. These six categories guided the entire system of mourning regulations in classical Chinese ritual law.

### The Six Principles

1. **親親** — Kinship affection: Mourning based on blood relationship. Closer blood ties require more elaborate mourning garments and longer periods.

2. **尊尊** — Hierarchical respect: Mourning based on social or ritual rank. One mourns superiors according to their position, regardless of direct blood ties.

3. **名** — Nomenclature/designation: Mourning obligations determined by naming conventions and relational categories (such as the designations for various in-law relationships).

4. **出入** — Entry and exit: Mourning for those entering (marrying into) or leaving (through marriage or adoption) the kinship group.

5. **長幼** — Elder and younger: Mourning gradations based on generational seniority. Older relatives receive more formal mourning than younger ones in equivalent relationships.

6. **從服** — Following the mourned: Mourning obligations that derive from one's connection to another person who is already in a mourning relationship.

### Complex "Following" Categories

The Daxizhuan further elaborates six sub-categories of "從服" (following the mourned):

1. **屬從** — Following through attachment: Mourning someone to whom you are related through a shared attachment.
2. **徒從** — Following through service: Mourning based on administrative or service relationships.
3. **從有服而無服** — Following to no mourning: Mourning someone who is in mourning but for whom you bear no mourning obligation.
4. **從無服而有服** — Following from no mourning to having mourning: Having mourning obligation for someone with whom you have no direct mourning tie.
5. **從重而輕** — Following from heavy to light: Gradually reducing mourning obligations.
6. **從輕而重** — Following from light to heavy: Gradually increasing mourning obligations.

### The Principle of Affection versus Righteousness

The Daxizhuan articulates a profound principle regarding the directionality of mourning obligations:

> "自仁率親，等而上之，至於祖，名曰輕。自義率祖，順而下之，至於禰，名曰重。"

This creates a paradox: upward movement through the lineage (from self to father to grandfather to great-grandfather to ancestor) is governed by affection (仁) and constitutes "lightening" obligations. Downward movement (from ancestor to great-grandfather to grandfather to father to self) is governed by righteousness (義) and constitutes "heavy" obligations.

The text concludes: "一輕一重，其義然也" (One light, one heavy—the principle is as stated), acknowledging this seeming contradiction while affirming its ritual correctness.

## 中文

《禮記·大傳》詳論服術六法，此乃喪服制度之理論總綱，決定了親疏尊卑之喪禮秩序。

### 服術六法

其一，**親親**。以血緣親疏定喪服之輕重。血緣愈近，喪服愈重，服期愈長。

其二，**尊尊**。以貴賤尊卑定喪服之禮數。位尊者雖非至親，亦當為之制服。

其三，**名**。以名分定喪服。如異姓之妇，依其夫之倫類而有名分之喪。

其四，**出入**。以外來加入或離去之關係定喪服。如娶入之妇、嫁出之女，皆有相應之服制。

其五，**長幼**。以年齒長幼定喪服之差等。年長者之喪，服制較年幼者為重。

其六，**從服**。因他人之喪而隨之有服。此乃衍生之喪服關係。

### 從服六別

《大傳》又分從服為六種：

- **屬從**：因附屬關係而從服。
- **徒從**：因徒屬關係而從服。
- **從有服而無服**：彼有服而我無服。
- **從無服而有服**：彼無服而我有服。
- **從重而輕**：由重服而至輕服。
- **從輕而重**：由輕服而至重服。

### 仁率親與義率祖

《大傳》提出一重要原則：

「自仁率親，等而上之，至於祖，名曰輕。自義率祖，順而下之，至於禰，名曰重。」

此言向上追祖先，由己身而父、而祖、而曾祖、而高祖，循親情之递减，是為「輕」；向下順祭祀，由始祖而曾祖、而祖、而父、而己，循義理之隆殺，是為「重」。輕重之間，恰相反覆，此聖人所以定服術之精義也。

## 日本語

『禮記·大傳』は服術六法を 제시し中國古代の喪服制度の理論的枠組みを構築する。

### 服術六法の内容

第一、**親親（しんしん）**。血縁の親疎によって喪服の轻重を決める。血縁が近いほど喪服は重く、着用期間も長くなる。

第二、**尊尊（そんそん）**。社会的地位に基づいて喪服を決める。位が上位の者にはたとえ直接の血縁がなくても服喪する。

第三、**名（めい）**。姻戚関係などの呼称に基づいて喪服を決める。

第四、**出入（しゅつにゅう）**。婚姻による加入や離開に基づく喪服関係。

第五、**長幼（ちょうよう）**。年齢的长幼に基づく喪服の差等。年長者の喪は年幼者より重い服制となる。

第六、**從服（じゅうふく）**。他者との繋がりから生じる派生的な服喪関係。

### 仁率親と義率祖

大伝は重要な原則を提示する：

「自仁率親、等而上之、至於祖、名曰軽。自義率祖、順而下之、至於禰、名曰重。」

上方に向かって祖先を追うにつれ親情は薄れ、これが「軽」である。下方に向かって祭祀を順にするにつれ義理は厚くなり、これが「重」である。この軽重の原理が服術制度の基礎となる。
