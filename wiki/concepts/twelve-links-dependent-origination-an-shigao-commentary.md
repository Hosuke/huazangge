## English

The **Twelve Links of Dependent Origination** (十二因緣, dvādaśāṅgapratītyasamutpāda) form a central doctrinal framework in An Shigao's commentary on the *Yin Chi Ru Jing*, representing the Buddhist analysis of the causes and conditions that perpetuate cyclic existence (samsara).

The commentary presents the twelve links in both forward (ignorance → aging-death) and reverse (cessation of ignorance → cessation of aging-death) orders, demonstrating the mechanistic nature of dependent origination:

**Forward Order:**
1. **Ignorance (癡/avidyā)**: "Ignorance is called not knowing the Four Noble Truths as they truly are." The commentary defines this as the fundamental unknowing that binds beings to the cycle, "like a blind person who cannot see safety or danger."

2. **Formations (行 saṃskāras)**: "From ignorance arise formations." Formations are the karmic impulses driven by ignorance that create the conditions for future existence.

3. **Consciousness (識/vijñāna)**: "From formations arise consciousness." When consciousness enters a womb, it immediately knows good and evil and develops feelings of attachment and aversion.

4. **Name-and-Form (名色/nāmarūpa)**: "From consciousness arises name-and-form." Name refers to the four non-form aggregates (feeling, perception, formations, consciousness), while form refers to the four great elements (earth, water, fire, air).

5. **Six Sense Bases (六入/ṣaḍāyatanas)**: "From name-and-form arise the six sense bases." The five aggregates give rise to the six sense faculties (eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind), which become doors for external objects to enter.

6. **Contact (觸/sparśa)**: "From the six sense bases arises contact." When the six faculties are complete, they bring about contact with the six external objects (form, sound, smell, taste, touch, dharmas).

7. **Sensation (痛/sparśa = vedanā)**: "From contact arises sensation." The commentary notes: "When one ages and the body becomes sick, life ends with painful sensations—foolish minds cling with love, speaking of it with choking voices."

8. **Craving (愛/trṣṇā)**: "From sensation arises craving." The attachment and thirst that arise from pleasant and unpleasant sensations drive further clinging.

9. **Clinging (取-upādāna)**: "From craving arises clinging." The commentary distinguishes four types: desire clinging (欲受), view clinging (見結受), precept-and-vow clinging (戒願受), and self-clinging (身結受).

10. **Existence (有/bhava)**: "From clinging arises existence." This refers to the three realms of existence: the desire realm, form realm, and formless realm.

11. **Birth (生/jāti)**: "From existence arises birth." With the completion of the three aggregates, consciousness takes on a new body in one of the five destinies.

12. **Aging and Death (老死/jarāmaraṇa)**: "From birth arise aging and death, worry, grief, suffering, and mental anguish." The commentary elaborates: "The great elements wither and die is aging; the spirit leaves and is transferred is death; internal burning heartache is worry; remembrance with sighs is grief; bearing heavy misfortune is suffering."

**Reverse Order:**
The commentary then shows the path to liberation: when ignorance is extinguished, formations are extinguished; when formations are extinguished, consciousness is extinguished—continuing through all twelve links until "aging and death, worry, grief, suffering, and mental anguish are extinguished. Thus the complete suffering aggregate is exhausted. When the twelve links of dependent origination are exhausted, that is Nirvāṇa."

The commentary emphasizes that understanding this causal chain is essential for liberation: "The wise one, seeing the twelve links, will thoroughly understand their desire and return to the path of cyclic existence, abandoning the body and receiving the body, accumulating immeasurable suffering."

## 中文

**十二因緣**（十二縁起）是安世高《陰持入經》註釋的核心教義之一，系統闡述了佛教對生死輪迴因果律的分析。

註釋以順逆兩種方式闡述十二因緣，揭示了緣起的機械性運作：

**順觀（流轉門）：**
1. **癡（無明/avidyā）**：註釋定義為「名為不知四諦如有」，即不能如實知見四聖諦的愚癡狀態，如同「盲者不明安危」。

2. **行（saṃskāras）**：由癡而起的身口意三業造作，形成未來投生的業力條件。

3. **識（vijñāna）**：註釋言「魂靈受身，即知好惡，而有憎愛之心也」。阿賴耶識投胎後即分別好醜。

4. **名色（nāmarūpa）**：「字，色也」——名為無形可睹的四非色陰（受想行識），色為可見的四大（地水火風）。

5. **六入（ṣaḍāyatanas）**：由五陰受身令六情生，「而有六邪入」。

6. **觸（sparśa）**：六情備，即致色聲香味細滑眾念。眼觸色、耳觸聲等。

7. **痛（vedanā/sensation）**：接觸外境所生之苦、樂、不苦不樂感受。註釋強調：「老至身病，命終毒痛，癡心戀愛。」

8. **愛（trṣṇā/craving）**：由感受生起之貪愛執著。

9. **取（upādāna/grasping）**：四取——欲取、見取、戒取、我取（身結受）。註釋詳述：「由愚者以不淨為淨，致受入欲淵；以苦為樂，致受入有淵。」

10. **有（bhava/existence）**：三有——欲界、色界、無色界。

11. **生（jāti/birth）**：已有盛陰，識神因親，受身更生，於五道中輪迴。

12. **老死（jarāmaraṇa）**：註釋云：「四大萎枯曰老，命盡神遷曰死，內熱心痛曰憂，存之噓唏曰悲，荷負重禍曰苦。」

**逆觀（還滅門）：**
「癡已盡便行盡，已行盡便識盡……已老死盡憂悲苦不可心惱便盡。如是具足苦種便得盡。」

當十二因緣盡，即泥洹（涅槃）矣。

## 日本語

**十二因縁**（十二縁起）は、安世高の『陰持入経』註釈における中核的教義框架である。生死流転の原因と条件に関する仏教的分析を体系的に阐述している。

註釈は順逆両面から十二因縁を阐述し、縁起の機械的性質を明らかにする：

**順観（流転門）：**
1. **癡（無明/avidyā）**：「四諦を如其分别知らぬことを名く」と定義される。盲者の如く安危を知らぬ状態。

2. **行（saṃskāras）**：癡に基づく身口意の業造作、未来の生存条件を創造する。

3. **識（vijñāna）**：「魂靈受身せば、即ち好惡を知りて、憎愛の心あり」——阿頼耶識が胞胎に入るや、直ちに善悪を分別す。

4. **名色（nāmarūpa）**：「字は色なり」——名は形無き四非色陰（受想行識）、色は四大（地水火風）。

5. **六入（ṣaḍāyatanas）**：「五陰受身して六情生じ、六邪入あり」。

6. **触（sparśa）**：「六情備はれば、即ち色聲香味細滑の衆念を致す」。

7. **痛（vedanā/受）**：「老いて身病し、命終れば毒痛あり、癡心は戀愛す」と註釈す。

8. **愛（trṣṇā/渇愛）**：「受より愛生ず」——快不快の受より貪愛執著が生ず。

9. **取（upādāna/執取）**：四取——欲取、見取、戒取、我取を詳述。

10. **有（bhava/存在）**：三有——欲界、色界、無色界。

11. **生（jāti/出生）**：「既に盛陰あれば、識神因りて親し、受身更生す」。

12. **老死（jarāmaraṇa）**：「四大枯萎して曰く老、命尽きて神遷る曰く死」と解説す。

**逆観（還滅門）：**
「癡已に尽きれば便ち行尽き、行已に尽きれば便ち識尽き……已に老死尽きて憂悲苦惱便ち尽き。如くは具足苦種便ち得盡」。

十二因縁尽きて、則ち泥洹（涅槃）なり。
