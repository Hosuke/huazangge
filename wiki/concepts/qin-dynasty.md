## English

### Overview
The Qin Dynasty (221–206 BC) was the first imperial dynasty of a unified China. It was founded after the conquest of the six rival states by the state of Qin, culminating in the campaigns led by the king Ying Zheng, who proclaimed himself Qin Shi Huang, "First Emperor of Qin". The dynasty established a centralized bureaucracy, standardized weights, measures, and a common script, and built massive infrastructure projects such as the Great Wall and a network of roads.

### Unification and Administration
Qin Shi Huang centralized power by abolishing the feudal system, dividing the empire into commanderies and counties administered by appointed officials. The legalist philosophy was adopted as the guiding ideology, emphasizing strict laws and harsh punishments to maintain order.

### Cultural Policy: The Burning of Books and Burying of Scholars
In 213 BC, the chancellor Li Si, acting on the emperor's orders, issued an edict to burn historical records and works of the Hundred Schools of Thought that were deemed subversive, except those dealing with law, agriculture, and medicine. Scholars who opposed the decree were reportedly buried alive (焚書坑儒), a notorious act that suppressed intellectual dissent and aimed at consolidating the emperor's authority.

### Major Achievements
- Construction of the Great Wall, linking existing fortifications to defend against northern nomadic invasions.
- Creation of the Terracotta Army, a massive mausoleum complex guarding the emperor's tomb.
- Standardization of the small seal script, facilitating communication across the empire.
- Development of an extensive road and canal system to improve trade and military logistics.

### Decline and Legacy
The dynasty lasted only fifteen years. The heavy taxes, forced labor, and brutal enforcement of laws sparked widespread revolts, culminating in the uprising of Chen Sheng and Wu Guang in 209 BC. After the emperor’s death in 210 BC, internal strife and the Chu–Han contention led to the collapse of Qin rule in 206 BC, succeeded by the Han Dynasty, which inherited many of Qin’s administrative structures, thus cementing the concept of a centralized imperial state.

## 中文

### 概述
秦朝（公元前221年—公元前206年）是中国历史上第一个统一的封建帝国，由秦始皇（赢政）统一六国后建立。秦始皇自称“始皇帝”，在统一后实行中央集权，推行统一的度量衡、文字和道路体系。

### 统一与行政制度
秦始皇废除分封制，设立郡县制，任命官员治理，以法家思想为治国之本，强调严刑峻法，以维持社会秩序。

### 焚书坑儒
公元前213年，丞相李斯奉秦始皇之命，下令焚烧《诗》、《书》等诸子百家的典籍，除法家、农业、医学等实用之书外一律焚毁。对敢于批评的儒生，则被活埋（焚书坑儒），以压制异见，巩固皇权。

### 主要成就
- 修建万里长城，联结北方防御线，抵御游牧民族侵扰。
- 建造秦始皇陵兵马俑，守护皇帝陵墓。
- 统一小篆文字，促进各地交流。
- 修筑驿道与运河，便利交通与军事调动。

### 衰亡与遗产
秦朝仅存十五年，因繁重的劳役、苛税与严酷法令激起民变。公元前209年，陈胜、吴广起义，随后公元前210年秦始皇逝世，权臣争斗，公元前206年秦朝灭亡，随后建立汉朝，继承并发展秦的行政制度，形成后世统一的中华帝国传统。

### 概要
秦王朝（紀元前221年 - 紀元前206年）は、中国初の中央集権的な統一王朝である。秦の始皇帝（秦始皇）が六カ国を滅ぼし、「最初の皇帝」を自称した後、帝国を統治した。

### 統一と行政
始皇帝は封建制を廃止し、郡県制を導入。法家思想に基づき、厳格な法律と刑罰で社会秩序を維持した。

### 焚書坑儒（ふんしょ こうじゅ）
