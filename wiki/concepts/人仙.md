## English

Renxian (人仙) literally means “human immortal” or “human celestial.” In early Buddhist literature it designates a person who has achieved a certain level of spiritual realization—typically the fruit of stream‑entry (Sotāpanna) or once‑returner (Sakadāgāmī)—and is subsequently reborn in a divine realm while still bearing a human name and form. The classic scriptural example appears in the *Renxian Sutra* (人仙經), which recounts the story of King Bimbisara of Magadha. After hearing the Buddha’s teachings, Bimbisara attains the fruit of stream‑entry and later is reborn as a son of the Four Heavenly Kings (四天王) in the celestial palace of the god Vaiśravaṇa (毘沙門天王). Because he retains his human designation yet dwells among devas, the texts call him “Renxian.”

### Origin and Scriptural Sources
- **Renxian Sutra (人仙經)** – a short Mahāyāna or early Buddhist sutra that narrates Bimbisara’s transformation.
- **References in the Bimbisara King of Magadha (頻婆娑羅王) article** – the sutra is quoted alongside the *Four Bases of Supernatural Power* (四神足) as a point of doctrinal connection.

### Theological Significance
1. **Intermediate States of Realization** – The notion of Renxian illustrates that progress on the Buddhist path does not require immediate full‑arhatship; even early fruits grant a supra‑human status.
2. **Human‑Divine Continuity** – By preserving a human name and identity in a celestial environment, Renxian underscores the continuity between mundane existence and transcendent states.
3. **Model for lay practice** – Bimbisara’s example demonstrates that a ruler, despite political responsibilities, can achieve spiritual merit and be reborn in a divine setting.

### Cosmological Context
Renxian reside in the **Cāturmahārājika** (Four Heavenly Kings) realm, the lowest of the six deva realms in the desire realm (kāmadhātu). This realm sits at the base of Mount Sumeru and is ruled by the four guardian kings. Inhabitants retain human‑like bodies, speech, and lifespan but enjoy far greater sensory pleasures and lifespans.

- **Deva (天人)** – general divine beings without a human origin.
- **Arhat (阿羅漢)** – a fully enlightened being who has ended the cycle of rebirth.
- **Bodhisattva (菩薩)** – a being aiming for full enlightenment for all beings.
