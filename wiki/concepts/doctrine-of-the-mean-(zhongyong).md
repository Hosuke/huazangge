## English

The **Doctrine of the Mean** (中庸, *Zhongyong*) is one of the Four Books of Confucianism, traditionally attributed to Confucius's grandson Zisi. The text articulates the principle of the "middle way"—not as mere compromise, but as the cultivation of equilibrium and harmony in moral conduct. Central to its teaching is the concept of *cheng* (誠, sincerity), which serves as the foundation from which proper human relationships and social order naturally emerge. The famous opening declares that the mean is the supreme virtue, distinguishing it from both excess and deficiency.

The text emphasizes that the junzi (君子, exemplary person) maintains their proper position through natural authenticity rather than artificial effort. As referenced in discussions of Liezi, the Zhongyong presents the ideal of "素其位而行" (acting according to one's proper station) as essential to moral cultivation. This balanced approach stands in interesting dialogue with Daoist critiques that view such儒家 conventions as ultimately limited.

The Zhongyong systematically develops the relationship between inner sincerity and outer harmonious action, showing how authentic self-cultivation naturally produces proper social conduct. The exemplary person embodies the mean through effortless alignment with moral principles, avoiding both extremes of behavior.

## 中文

**中庸**是儒家《四書》之一，相傳由孔子之孫子思所述。該書闡述「中庸」之道，非指折衷妥協，而在於培養行為之適度與和諧。其核心概念為「誠」，視誠為一切道德修養與社會秩序之根基。開篇即言：「天命之謂性，率性之謂道，修道之謂教。」

中庸強調君子當「素其位而行」，即依本分而行，不逾矩。此說與道家對儒家工夫之批判形成對話，《列子》中「無樂無知」之境界正對此提出反思與深化。君子之道「淡而不厭」，平淡中見恆久，體現真正的修養境界。

中庸系統地論述真誠與外在行為之和諧關係，說明內心真誠自能產生端正之社會行為。聖人境界在於無過不及，自然合乎道德原則，達到天人合一之圓滿狀態。

## English

The **Doctrine of the Mean** (中庸, *Zhōngyōng*) is one of the core texts of the Confucian canon, traditionally attributed to Zisi (子思), the grandson of Confucius. As one of the [[Four Books]] of Confucianism, it systematizes the Master's teachings on maintaining equilibrium between extremes. The text articulates how proper moral cultivation requires staying within proper bounds—not excess, not deficiency—while preserving the unity between human nature and the cosmic order. This principle of the "middle way" extends from personal ethics to political governance, forming a bridge between individual self-cultivation and cosmic harmony.

The text's central concern lies in the nature of [[Human nature]] and the path to becoming a "superior person" (君子, *jūnzǐ*). It emphasizes *cheng* (誠, sincerity or authenticity) as the fundamental virtue from which all other moral qualities flow. By cultivating inner sincerity, one aligns oneself with the patterns of Heaven, achieving a state where personal virtue and cosmic order become unified. This framework deeply informed later developments in [[Confucian Political Philosophy]], particularly concepts like the "Great Commonwealth" (大同) found in the *Book of Rites*.

The [[Doctrine of the Mean]] became especially influential during the Song Dynasty when Zhu Xi elevated it to canonical status as part of the Four Books. Later thinkers such as Wang Yangming drew upon its emphasis on inner moral intuition. Its treatment of how individual cultivation relates to broader cosmic and political order made it a cornerstone text for understanding how Confucian ideals could be applied across both personal and governmental domains.

## 中文

**中庸**是儒家经典的核心文本之一，传统上被认为由孔子的孙子子思所著。作为[[四书]]之一，它系统化了孔子关于在极端之间保持平衡的教导。这部著作阐述了正当的道德修养如何要求保持在适当的范围内——既不过度，也不欠缺——同时保持人性与宇宙秩序的统一。这种"中道"原则从个人道德延伸到政治治理，形成了个人修养与宇宙和谐之间的桥梁。

《中庸》文本的核心关切在于[[人性]]的本质以及成为"君子"的修养路径。它强调"诚"作为根本美德，其他所有道德品质都从中衍生。通过培养内心的真诚，人们与天的规律保持一致，达到个人美德与宇宙秩序统一的境界。这一框架深刻影响了后来[[儒家政治哲学]]的发展，特别是《礼记》中"大同"概念的形成。

中庸在宋代获得了特别的影响力，朱熹将其提升为四书的重要地位。王阳明等后来的思想家则借鉴了其对内在道德直觉的强调。由于它论述了个体修养如何与更广泛的宇宙秩序和政治秩序相联系，因此成为理解儒家理想如何应用于个人和国家治理领域的核心文本。
