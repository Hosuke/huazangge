## English

The Ten Remembrances (十念) is a Buddhist meditation practice systematized in the "Commentary on Classifying Merit" (分別功德論), traditionally attributed to the dispensation of Ananda's compilation of the Buddhist canon. This practice provides practitioners with ten distinct objects of contemplation, progressing from external recollections to internal analytical meditations.

The ten remembrances are: (1) mindfulness of the Buddha, (2) mindfulness of the Dharma, (3) mindfulness of the Saṅgha, (4) mindfulness of the precepts, (5) mindfulness of giving, (6) mindfulness of heavenly realms, (7) mindfulness of cessation, (8) mindfulness of the body, (9) mindfulness of death, and (10) mindfulness of the single doctrine.

**Mindfulness of the Buddha (念佛)** involves contemplating the Buddha's thirty-two major and eighty minor marks, his golden-colored body that radiates light, and his footprints that can resurrect dead insects and heal the afflicted. A particularly striking account describes a monk who tried to defame the Buddha by placing dead insects on the Buddha's footprints—yet the insects were resurrected, demonstrating the Buddha's extraordinary spiritual power.

**Mindfulness of the Dharma (念法)** concerns the "no-defilement Dharma" (無漏法), the "desireless Dharma" (無欲法), the "path Dharma" (道法), and the "unconditioned Dharma" (無為法). The text explains that while the Dharma is primary ("Dharma produced the Buddhas, Dharma generates the Buddha-way"), the Buddha must be contemplated first because the Dharma, though supreme, requires a awakened being to reveal and explain it. The text uses the metaphor of treasure hidden underground: although the treasure exists everywhere, only one with spiritual power can indicate its location and enable others to benefit.

**Mindfulness of the Saṅgha (念僧)** concerns the four pairs and eight individuals (四雙八輩), the twelve worthy ones (十二賢士), who have abandoned worldly entanglement and serve as the "good field of blessings for beings" (眾生良祐福田). The text illustrates this with the story of the unfortunate monk Bǎluódá (梵摩達), who was so deficient in merit that food placed in his bowl would magically disappear. Only after the Buddha caused two monks practicing the meditative absorption of cessation (滅盡定) to be present, and then arranged for monks practicing the four immeasurables (四等—loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity) to surround him, did his merit restore and he could receive sustenance. Birds and beasts were also unable to harm him because of the collective merit of the Saṅgha.

**Mindfulness of the Precepts (念戒)** concerns the five precepts, the ten precepts, the 250 precepts, and the 500 precepts—all serving to restrain the body, guard the six sense faculties, and cut off desires. The text distinguishes between two monks traveling together: one who died rather than drink water filled with insects, thereby being reborn in a heavenly realm, and another who drank the water to preserve his life but was rebuked by the Buddha for prioritizing physical survival over the purity of his precepts. The text also distinguishes between "householder precepts" (俗戒) such as the five precepts and ten wholesome deeds, and "path precepts" (道戒) such as the three samādhis and the four truths' wisdom.

**Mindfulness of Giving (念施)** encompasses both material giving (財施) and Dharma giving (法施). Through material giving one reaches Dharma giving, completing the perfection of giving (檀度無極) and attaining nirvāṇa.

**Mindfulness of Heavenly Realms (念天)** concerns the desire realm heavens, the form realm heavens, and the formless realm heavens. These are distinguished between "blessing-receiving heavens" (受福天) and "moral-heaven" (道德天). The text notes that those in the desire realm who have attained the stage of the stream-enterer (須陀洹) have permanently escaped the three evil realms and progress toward the path hall; those in the form and formless realms cultivate meditation and achieve uncontaminated liberation.

**Mindfulness of Cessation (念休息)** means the mind's thoughts cease, the five desires do not arise, and one becomes eternally still. The text illustrates with the story of a Brahmin who sat in meditation under a tree, but whose true intention was to become a king. The monk Sūrottara (須羅陀) appeared and, through spiritual power, transformed into a chicken, demonstrating to the Brahmin that "killing识" (the desire for domination) was the obstacle preventing genuine rest.

**Mindfulness of the Body (念身)** involves contemplating the body's thirty-six impure substances and foul secretions. A memorable account describes a monk eating by a river who noticed a beautiful woman's hair falling into his bowl. He became obsessed with imagining her beauty, only to discover later that she had been partially devoured by foxes and wolves. This taught him the impermanence of physical beauty and attachment, leading him to stream-entry.

**Mindfulness of Death (念死)** involves contemplating that life is transient, the sense faculties decay like rotting wood, and the life-root is cut off. The text recounts the monk Póujílí (婆吉梨), who after years of meditation yet still suffering from defilements, decided to cut his own throat to achieve nirvāṇa—since the body was the obstacle, removing it would enable the formless. Upon cutting his throat, he instantly attained arhatship. When the Buddha heard of this, he instructed the monks to perform the funeral rites.

The final tenth recollection—mindfulness of the single doctrine (念一法)—concerns contemplating the fundamental principles of Buddhist doctrine, serving as a comprehensive synthesis of the teachings.

According to the text, the Buddha first taught the ten remembrances in summary to quick-witted beings, and only later elaborated each one individually for those of slower capacity.

## 中文

十念是佛教中的一種禪修法門，見於《分別功德論》。此法門為修行者提供十個不同的專念對象，從外部的憶念逐步過渡到內部的分析觀照。

十念依次為：念佛、念法、念僧、念戒、念施、念天、念休息、念身、念死、念一法。

**念佛**涉及觀想佛陀的三十二相、八十種好，以及其金色之身所放射的光明。經文特別記載了一個惡比丘試圖誹謗佛陀，將死去的蟲蟻置於佛足跡處——然而這些蟲蟻竟復活過來，顯示了佛陀超凡的靈力。又記佛行時足離地面四寸，千輻相輪之紋現於地上，足下蟲蟻七日安隱，若命終者皆得生天。

**念法**涉及無漏法、無欲法、道法、無為法。經文以伏藏為喻：地下雖有珍寶，然非神通之人不能指示其處。經云「法出諸佛、法生佛道」，然法雖微妙無能知者，必須如來開示，故念佛在先。

**念僧**涉及四雙八輩、十二賢士。經文以梵摩達比丘為例：此比丘福報極薄，飲食入缽自然消化。佛令具滅盡定之比丘在其左右，又令修四無量心之比丘依次圍繞，由於眾僧福力，鳥獸不能侵害，其缽食始得保全。

**念戒**涉及五戒、十戒、二百五十戒乃至五百戒。經文以二比丘共行為喻：其一寧死不飲充满蟲蟻之水，命終即生天上；其一飲水全命，卻被佛責備，以其全戒之功即生天上，而此人雖見佛，去教甚遠。經文又別俗戒與道戒：五戒十善為俗戒，三三昧為道戒。

**念施**包括財施與法施，由財施得達法施，成檀度無極，故得至涅槃。

**念天**涉及欲界、色界乃至無色界天。經文區分受福天與道德天：須陀洹天永離三惡趣；色界空界八淨居天增修止觀，進成無漏，即彼涅槃不還世間。

**念休息**即心意想息，五欲不起，寂然永定。經文以鴦掘魔羅梵志為喻：此梵志坐禪時心存求作國王之念，須羅陀比丘化作雞以示其心之「識雞」，使其悟知殺心之本質，方得名為真正休息。

**念身**涉及觀身三十六物不淨惡露。經文記載比丘因見女人頭髮而起欲念，循水追求欲見其人，卻發現彼女已被狐狼噉食半身，爛壞臭穢。由是悟身不淨，即得道跡。

**念死**涉及念命逝不停、諸根散壞如腐敗木。經文載婆吉梨比丘因身纏綿流轉而欲自刎求涅槃，刎頭之際即得阿羅漢。

第十念**念一法**涉及觀想佛教基本教理，為十念之總結。經文稱前十念佛總說為利根眾生，後更說者為鈍根眾生析解其義。

## 日本語

十念（じゅうねん）は、仏教の瞑想実践の一つで、『分別功德論』に記載される。この修行法では、十个の異なる объекта想念の対象が体系化されている。

十念とは：(1)仏を念ずること、(2)法を念ずること、(3)僧を念ずること、(4)戒を念ずること、(5)施を念ずること、(6)天を念ずること、(7)休息を念ずること、(8)身を念ずること、(9)死を念ずること、(10)一法を念ずることである。

**念仏**は、仏陀の三十二相八十種好を瞑想することを含む。経典には、仏の足形に死んだ虫を置いても蘇生するという記述がある。

**念法**は、無漏法・無欲法・道法・無為法を瞑想する。地下の宝物のような喩えを用いて、仏が法を説く必要があると説明する。

**念僧**は、四双八輩十二賢士を想念し、衆生の良福田であることを理解する。福徳の薄い梵摩達比丘の物語が引用され、衆僧の功徳力がいかにして彼を守ったかが描かれる。

**念戒**は、五戒・十戒・二百五十戒・五百戒を想い、身体と六根を 制禦することを含む。二比丘の物語では、一方が虫のいる水を飲まずに命を落とすことで天に生まれ変わる様が描かれている。

**念施**は、財施と法施を含み、布施の功徳完成によって涅槃に達するとされる。

**念天**は、欲界天・色界天・無色界天を想念する。聖者の天と凡夫天的区别が強調される。

**念休息**は、心の想念が止み、五欲不起となって永遠に寂静に至ることを意味する。梵志が国王になろうとする心の「識鶏」を殺す故事が引用される。

**念身**は、身体の三十六種の不浄物を観察することを含む。美しき女の髪が水和而上流に漂い、それが狐狼に喰われた死骸の髪であることを発見した比丘の物語が含まれる。

**念死**は、命が瞬く間に過ぎ去り、六根が腐った木のように壊れることを想念する。婆吉梨比丘が自害して阿羅漢果を得た故事が引用される。
