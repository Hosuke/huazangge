## English

The Three Treasures (also known as the Three Jewels or Three Gems) represent the most fundamental objects of refuge in Buddhism. They consist of the Buddha (the teacher), the Dharma (the teaching), and the Sangha (the community of practitioners). In Buddhist doctrine, these three are regarded as precious and worthy of veneration, providing the foundation for Buddhist practice and spiritual liberation.

In the context of the Four Debts of Gratitude (四恩), the kindness of the Three Treasures is considered "inconceivable" (不思議), providing benefit and happiness to all beings without rest. Practitioners acknowledge their profound debt to these three sources of spiritual guidance, which offer the teachings and community support necessary for progressing along the Buddhist path.

The concept appears prominently in texts such as the Gratitude Sutra (報恩經), where the Buddha elaborates on the immeasurable kindness of the Three Treasures. Taking refuge in the Three Treasures marks the formal entry into Buddhist practice and represents a lifelong commitment to following the Buddha's teachings with the support of the monastic community.

## 中文

三宝（傳統中文寫作三寶）是佛教最根本的皈依對象，包括佛（覺者）、法（教法）和僧（修行者團體）。這三者被視為珍貴且值得恭敬的對象，是佛教修行與精神解脫的基礎。

在四恩的思想框架中，三寶之恩被認為是「不思議」的，即不可思議的。三寶無有休息地利益眾生、給予安樂。修行者承認自己對這三個精神導師的深刻恩情，正是他們提供了修行道路上所需的法義與團體支持。

三寶的概念在《報恩經》等典籍中尤為突出，佛陀詳細闡述了三寶的無量恩德。皈依三寶標誌著正式進入佛教修行，代表著在僧團的支持下終身奉行佛法。
