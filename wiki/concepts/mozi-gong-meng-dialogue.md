## English

The chapter "Gong Meng" (公孟) in the Mozi presents a series of debates between Mozi and a Confucian scholar named Gong Meng. These dialogues constitute one of the most important texts for understanding Mozi's critique of Confucian doctrine and his articulation of Mohist philosophy.

The dialogue opens with Gong Meng presenting the bell analogy: "A gentleman awaits questions before speaking, and remains silent otherwise. It is like a bell—when struck, it sounds; when not struck, it is silent." Mozi counters with the urgency argument: when a ruler is committing injustices, one must speak out regardless of being asked. This establishes the central Mohist principle that moral persons must actively intervene when they witness wrongdoing.

**The Four Harmful Doctrines (四害)**

Mozi's most systematic critique appears in his condemnation of four Confucian teachings:

1. **Denial of Heaven and Ghosts**: The Confucian view that Heaven does not regulate human affairs and that ghosts and spirits have no awareness, Mozi argues, removes the supernatural basis for moral order.

2. **Elaborate Burial and Extended Mourning**: The Confucian practice of constructing multiple coffins, providing abundant clothing for the dead, treating funeral processions like relocations, three years of weeping, supporting oneself with walking sticks—all these, Mozi contends, harm both the living and the dead.

3. **Musical Culture**: The Confucian emphasis on弦歌鼓舞 (stringed music and drumming), Mozi identifies as contributing to the decline of social order.

4. **Fatalism (命)**: The belief that wealth, poverty, longevity, and brevity are fixed by fate and cannot be altered, Mozi sees as destroying human agency and diligence.

**The Conduct-Dress Debate**

When Gong Meng argues that "a gentleman must use ancient words and clothing before he can be benevolent," Mozi provides historical counterexamples: Duke Huan of Qi wore tall caps and broad sashes with metal swords and wooden shields—yet his state was well-governed. Duke Wen of Jin wore coarse cloth garments and sheepskin with swords—yet his state prospered. King Zhuang of Chu wore elaborate crown and voluminous robes—yet his state was ordered. King Goujian of Yue shaved his head and tattooed his body—yet his state was well-governed. Mozi concludes: "Conduct lies not in clothing."

**Action and Personal Governance**

The chapter concludes with Mozi's insistence on the unity of words and actions: "Those who govern the state speak with their mouths and must act with their bodies. If you speak with your mouth but do not act with your body, this is disorder in your own person. If you cannot govern your own person, how can you govern state affairs?"

This dialogue demonstrates Mozi's characteristic method: using historical examples, logical inference, and practical consequences to demolish Confucian positions and establish his own utilitarian ethics.

## 中文

《墨子·公孟》篇記載了墨子與儒家學者公孟子之間的一系列辯論，是理解墨子批判儒家學說及墨家哲學的重要文本。本篇涉及政治哲學、倫理學、天命觀、鬼神論及修身觀等多個層面。

** bell analogy 與主動進諫之辯**

公孟子以「君子共己以待，問焉則言，不問焉則止」比喻鐘聲「扣則鳴，不扣則不鳴」，主張君子在被動詢問時才發言。墨子則以「若大人行淫暴於國家，進而諫則謂之不遜」為據，強調當統治者施行暴政時，賢士必須主動進諫，即使未受詢問亦當發聲。此辯論確立了墨家「有難則救」的核心原則。

**四害：墨子對儒家的系統批判**

墨子對儒家的批判集中於四個面向：

其一為「不明鬼神」：儒家以天為不明、以鬼為不神，墨子認為此說剝奪了賞善罰惡的超自然根據，故《明鬼》篇力證鬼神實有且能降禍福。

其二為「厚葬久喪」：墨子批評儒家「重為棺椁，多為衣衾，送死若徙，三年哭泣，扶後起，杖後行，耳無聞，目無見」，此俗既耗費資源又妨礙生產，為治國之大害。

其三為「弦歌鼓舞」：儒家重視音樂教化，墨子則視其為無益於生計之事。

其四為「執有命」：儒家相信「貧富壽夭，齰然在天，不可損益」，墨子斥之為使人怠惰之說，主張「強必富，不強必貧；強必貴，不強必賤」。

**言行關係之辯**

告子謂「我治國為政」，墨子答以「政者，口言之，身必行之。今子口言之而身不行，是子之身亂也。子不能治子之身，惡能治國政？」此語直接點出墨家「言行一致」的修身要求。

## 日本語

『墨子·公孟』篇は、墨子と儒家学者公孟子との一連の対話を通じて、墨家の思想と儒家思想の相違を明らかにする重要篇章である。本篇では、政治倫理運命論、鬼神論、衣装と行為の関係、修身の方法など、多面的な哲学的問題が論じられている。

**鐘の比喩と能動的進言**

公孟子 initially presents a passive stance of the "gentleman" who waits to be asked before speaking, using the metaphor that a bell only sounds when struck. Mozi counters with the necessity of active remonstrance when rulers commit injustices, establishing the Mohist principle of rescuing those in danger without waiting to be summoned.

**四つの害悪：儒家思想への体系的批判**

墨子は儒家の四つの主要な教えを批判する：

第一に「鬼神を不明視する」立場。儒家が天と鬼神の支配力を否定するならば、賞善罰悪の超自然的基盤が失われると墨子は主張する。

第二に「厚葬久喪」の慣習。多層の棺桶、豊富な副葬品、三年間の mourning哭、杖による歩行支援、耳聾と盲目状態を人工的に作り出す這些の慣習は、国家の資源を浪費し、生産性を損なうと批判する。

第三に「音楽礼讃」。儒家の音楽教育を重視する態度を、子弟の実用的発達の妨げとして退ける。

第四に「運命論」。富貴貧賤が天によって固定され、人間の努力では変更できないという信念を、怠惰と無責任を招くもの」として痛烈に批判する。

**言行一致の原則**

墨子は「仁義を說く者は、必ず身をもって行之べし」という原則を強調する。言葉と行動の統一を求め、特に統治者に対してはその身を治めることが国を治める前提條件であると論じた。
