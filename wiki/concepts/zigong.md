## English

Zigong (子贡), also known as Duanmu Ci (端木赐), was one of the most distinguished disciples of [[Confucius]]. Unlike some of his fellow disciples who focused primarily on moral or political philosophy, Zigong was renowned for his exceptional diplomatic abilities and commercial acumen, which set him apart among the [[Analects-disciples|disciples of Confucius]].

Historical records and the [[Analects]] depict Zigong as a man of considerable intellect and practical wisdom. He engaged with Confucius on profound philosophical questions, notably asking insightful questions about [[Ren]] (仁, benevolence/humaneness) as referenced in Analects 6.30. His approach to learning combined theoretical understanding with practical application.

Zigong's legacy extends beyond philosophical discourse; his success as a merchant and diplomat demonstrated how Confucian ideals could be applied in the commercial and political spheres. Together with disciples like [[Zilu]], he represented different aspects of the diverse talents within Confucius's inner circle of students.

## 中文

子贡（前520—前456），复姓端木，名赐，字子贡，是[[孔子]]门下最杰出的[[Analects-disciples|弟子]]之一。与其他专注于道德或政治哲学的同门不同，子贡以出色的外交才能和商业头脑著称，这在孔门弟子中较为罕见。

《论语》及历史记载显示，子贡兼具卓越智慧与务实精神。他曾就[[仁]]的概念向孔子请教，[[论语]]6.30篇记载了他提出的深刻问题。他的学习方法将理论理解与实践应用相结合。

子贡的贡献不仅限于哲学讨论；作为商人和外交家，他的成功证明了儒家理想可以应用于商业和政治领域。与[[Zilu|子路]]等弟子一起，他代表了孔子弟子群体中不同才能与志向的多元面向。
