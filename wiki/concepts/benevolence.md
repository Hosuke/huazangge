## English

**Benevolence** (仁) is a cornerstone of Confucian ethics, representing an essential quality that individuals should cultivate in their interactions with others. Confucius articulates that benevolence is not merely a feeling but requires action and commitment to the well-being of others. He states, "可与言而不与之言，失人；不可与言而与之言，失言," indicating that benevolence involves both the willingness to communicate and the duty to maintain integrity in one’s words.

Benevolence extends beyond mere kindness; it informs the moral compass of individuals and guides their actions in social contexts. Confucius poses that successful governance is built on the foundation of benevolence. Leaders are expected to embody this virtue, demonstrating empathy and care for their citizens. The claim that "君子治道，君子者，仁政" reinforces the notion that democracy and justice in governance stem from compassionate leadership.

Furthermore, the text underscores the importance of benevolence in education and personal relationships. Confucius emphasizes, "人之於仁也，甚於水火," portraying benevolence as a necessity for survival. It suggests that, just as one cannot survive without water and fire, societies cannot thrive without benevolence. This understanding enforces the responsibility individuals have towards each other.

In summary, benevolence is central to maintaining harmonious human relationships and fostering a just society, reflecting the core values of Confucianism.

## 中文

仁超越了单纯的善良；它构成了个体的道德方向感，并指导他们在社会环境中的行为。孔子认为，成功的治理是建立在仁的基础之上的。领导者被期待体现这一美德，向公民展示同情心和关怀。“君子治道，君子者，仁政”这一说法进一步强化了仁爱的领导与民主公正之间的密切联系。

此外，文本还强调了仁在教育和人际关系中的重要性。孔子强调：“人之於仁也，甚於水火”，将仁视为生存的必需品。这表明，正如人们无法在没有水和火的情况下生存，社会也无法在没有仁的环境中蓬勃发展。这一认识加强了个体对彼此的责任感。

综上所述，仁在维持和谐的人际关系和促进公正社会方面处于核心地位，反映了儒教的核心价值。

## English

# Benevolence (Ren 仁)

**Benevolence** (仁, rén) is the supreme virtue in Confucian philosophy, representing humaneness, compassion, and perfect virtue. It encompasses the ideal relationship between humans and serves as the foundation of ethical conduct.

## Fundamental Nature

Confucius identifies filial piety as the root of benevolence: "孝弟也者，其為仁之本與!" (Filial piety and fraternal duty—are they not the root of benevolence?) This establishes family relationships as the training ground for developing broader human compassion.

## Authenticity Over Appearance

The Master warns against superficial displays: "巧言令色，鮮矣仁!" (Fine words and an insinuating appearance are seldom associated with true virtue). Genuine [[benevolence]] requires substance over form, contrasting with mere ritual observance.

## Cultivation Through Practice

Benevolence is developed through concrete actions: "弟子入則孝，出則弟，謹而信，汎愛眾，而親仁" (A young man should be filial at home, respectful abroad, conscientious and truthful, overflowing in love for all, and intimate with those who are [[benevolent]]).

## Connection to Other Virtues

[[Benevolence]] integrates with faithfulness, ritual propriety, and wisdom. It informs the character of the [[junzi]] and underpins [[benevolent-government]]. The practice of [[confucian-self-examination]] helps maintain this virtue.

# 仁爱

**仁爱**（仁）是儒家哲学中的至高美德，代表人道、同情和完美德行。它涵盖人与人之间的理想关系，是伦理行为的基础。

## 根本性质

孔子将孝悌视为仁的根本：「孝弟也者，其為仁之本與！」这确立了家庭关系作为培养更广泛人类同情心的训练场。

## 实质重于表象

夫子警告表面表演：「巧言令色，鮮矣仁！」真正的[[仁爱]]需要实质而非形式，与单纯的礼仪遵守形成对比。

## 通过实践培养

仁通过具体行动发展：「弟子入則孝，出則弟，謹而信，汎愛眾，而親仁」表明年轻人应在家孝顺、在外恭敬、谨慎诚实、博爱众人、亲近[[仁者]]。

## 与其他美德的关系

[[仁爱]]与忠信、礼义、智慧相融合。它塑造[[君子]]品格并支撑[[仁政]]。[[儒家自省]]的实践有助于保持这一美德。

## English

### The Nature of Benevolence

Benevolence (仁, rén) stands as the foundational virtue in Confucian ethics, representing the highest moral achievement and the essence of human excellence. As Confucius states: "The Master said, 'Is benevolence indeed so far away? If we really wanted benevolence, we should find that it was at our very side'" (Analects 7.30).

In the "Li Ren" chapter, Confucius elaborates: "The Master said, 'It is benevolence that makes a locality beautiful. If a man in selecting a residence does not fix upon one where benevolence prevails, how can he be wise?'" This establishes benevolence as both an environmental quality and a personal attainment.

### Characteristics of the Benevolent Person

The benevolent individual exhibits distinctive qualities:
- "The benevolent are free from anxiety; the wise are free from perplexity; the courageous are free from fear" (Analects 9.29)
- "The Master said, 'It is only the benevolent who can love others or hate others'" (Analects 4.3)
- "The Master said, 'If the will be set on benevolence, there will be no practice of wickedness'" (Analects 4.4)

### Cultivation and Practice

Benevolence requires constant cultivation: "The Master said, 'If a superior man abandon benevolence, how can he fulfill the requirements of that name? The superior man does not, even for the space of a single meal, act contrary to benevolence. In moments of haste, he cleaves to it. In seasons of danger, he cleaves to it'" (Analects 4.5).

The relationship between [[virtue-ethics]] and [[confucian-ethics]] demonstrates how benevolence integrates with other virtues like righteousness and propriety to form a complete moral character.

## 中文

### 仁之本質

仁乃儒家倫理之核心美德，代表道德修養之最高境界與人性之完美體現。孔子曰：「里仁為美。擇不處仁，焉得知？」（《論語·里仁》）此揭示仁不僅為個人品德，亦為環境之理想狀態。

### 仁者之特質

具仁德者表現出獨特品格：
- 「唯仁者能好人，能惡人」（《論語·里仁》）
- 「苟志於仁矣，無惡也」（《論語·里仁》）
- 「不仁者不可以久處約，不可以長處樂。仁者安仁，知者利仁」（《論語·里仁》）

### 仁德之修養

仁德需持續修持：「君子去仁，惡乎成名？君子無終食之間違仁，造次必於是，顛沛必於是」（《論語·里仁》）。此與[[virtue-ethics]]及[[confucian-ethics]]相呼應，顯示仁與義、禮等美德共同構成完整道德人格。
