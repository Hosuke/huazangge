## English

The present text, constituting Volume 14 of Ge Hong's (抱朴子) Inner Chapters (內篇), constitutes a sustained meditation upon the fundamental significance of discovering authentic spiritual masters within the Daoist cultivation tradition. Ge Hong articulates a profound recognition that while the great virtue of Heaven and Earth lies in the generation of life (天地之大德曰生), the most secret and precious teachings of the Daoist masters—namely, the methods of achieving immortality (長生之方)—are transmitted only through a process of rigorous selection and initiation.

The text emphasizes that the transmission of these supreme teachings follows a carefully delineated protocol: the blood covenant ritual (血盟乃傳) ensures that profound secrets are shared exclusively with worthy recipients, with violations of this sacred trust incurring divine punishment (戒在天罰). As Ge Hong observes, "Prior teachers dared not casually transmit these teachings to people; they awaited those who sought them most diligently, and even then selected only the most refined individuals for instruction."

A central concern of this chapter involves the problem of false practitioners who appropriate the name of Daoist masters (竊道士之號者) without possessing genuine understanding. These imposters "merely open their mouths slightly, employing false pretensions of名声 and engaging in deceptive practices, yet their duped followers cannot distinguish truth from falsehood." Their presence, Ge Hong warns, significantly impedes sincere seekers who may abandon their quest upon encountering such charlatans.

The narrative presents several exemplary cases of proper master-disciple relationships, including the legendary Zhang Liang (張良), who received instruction from the hermit of Tusong Mountain (圯上老人), and Chen Anshi (陳安世), a thirteen-year-old youth who became the teacher of the elderly Guan Shuben (灌叔本), despite his youth. In the latter case, Shuben reportedly bowed daily to Anshi, declaring: "The Way is honored and virtue is precious; the one who first attains the Dao becomes the teacher—I dare not be weary of performing the disciple's rites." This inversion of conventional social hierarchies illustrates Ge Hong's fundamental proposition that genuine understanding, not age or status, determines the proper master-disciple relationship.

The text concludes with an impassioned appeal for the cultivation of proper teacher-seeking attitudes, noting that "the kindness of a bright master surpasses even that of Heaven and Earth, and is certainly weightier than the kindness of parents—how could one fail to honor this? How could one fail to seek it?"

## 中文

抱朴子卷十四為葛洪《內篇》之重要篇章，集中論述了道教修仙傳統中求訪明師之要義。本文開篇即以「天地之大德曰生」為立論根基，揭示道家最秘最重之學問——長生之方——之傳授法則。葛洪指出，此等至道「血盟乃傳」，非其人不授，違者遭天罰，且先師必待求之至勤、揀選至精者方肯教之。

本卷詳論世間偽道之弊病。葛洪指出：「世間自有奸偽圖錢之子，而竊道士之號者，不可勝數。」此輩「粗開頭角，或妄沽名」，而好事之徒不能辨別真偽，「徒多之進問，自取誑惑」，遂令有志者息遠求之意。此輩以毀賢嫉能為事，「恐彼聲名之過己」，實則「圖其財力，以快其情欲而已」。

文中列舉張良受圯上老人之教、陳安年十三歲而為灌叔本之師等例，說明「道尊德貴，先得道者則為師」之理。灌叔本年七十而朝夕拜安世曰：「道尊德貴，先得道者則為師矣，吾不敢倦執弟子之禮也。」此打破世俗年齡階層之見，彰显真道传授不以名位为据。

葛洪最後感歎：「明師之恩，誠為過於天地，重於父母多矣，可不崇之乎？可不求之乎？」呼籲學者當勤求明師，而非自恃聰明、守枯待兔。

## 日本語

抱朴子第十四巻は、葛洪の『内篇』における重要な章であり、道教修仙伝統における真の師を求めることの根本的な意義について論じている。本テキストは、「天地の大徳は生を曰う」という思想的基础上、長生の方という最も秘密で貴重な道教の教えの伝授方法について考察している。

血盟の儀式を通じてのみ伝わるこれらの教えは、真に德を積んだ者のみが受け取ることができる。葛洪は、偽りの道士が道を窃取し、虚飾の名声を求める問題を深く指摘している。彼らは真理を見分ける能力を持たず、有志の追求を阻む存在である。

張良と圯上老人、陳安世と灌叔本などの具体例は、年齢や地位に関係なく、真の理解が師弟関係の基準となることを示している。最終的には、真の師の恩寵は天地父母的恩寵さえ超えるものであり、その追求努力的重要性を強調している。
