## English

The *Shi Zi Yue Fo Ben Sheng Jing* (師子月佛本生經), or "The Buddha's Discourse on the Lion-Moon Buddha's Previous Lives," is a Buddhist Jataka tale that illustrates the principles of [[shan-e-bao-ying-karmic-retribution|karmic retribution]], the transformative power of taking [[three-treasures|the Three Refuges]] and the [[five-hindrances-ten-fetters-buddhist-doctrine|Five Precepts]], and the bodhisattva path leading to buddhahood.

### Narrative Structure

The sutra opens with the Buddha residing at the Bamboo Grove in Rajagriha with 1,250 bhikkhus and 100 bodhisattvas. Among them is the bhikkhu Vasumitra (婆須蜜多), known for his extraordinary behavior—climbing trees like a monkey, making sounds like apes, and performing various entertainments. This behavior scandalizes King Bimbisara (頻婆娑羅王), who visits the Buddha to inquire about these unusual monks.

The Buddha reveals the elaborate karmic history of Vasumitra. In a past life during the time of a distant Buddha, a monkey encountered an [[arhat-stages-buddhist-liberation|arhat]] meditating in the forest. Imitating monastic behavior, the monkey donned the monk's robes and carried incense. The arhat, seeing the animal's spiritual potential, initiated it into [[three-treasures|the Three Refuges]] and administered the [[five-hindrances-ten-fetters-buddhist-doctrine|Five Precepts]], particularly emphasizing the precept against killing.

This single act of [[three-poisons-greed-hatred-delusion-buddhist-teaching|meritorious confession]] transformed the monkey's karmic trajectory. Though it died shortly after from a fall, it was reborn in the Trayastrimsa Heaven (三十三天) and eventually as a human. Over countless lifetimes, it encountered many Buddhas, ultimately fulfilling the [[bu-chu-yi-sheng-yu-chu|one life remaining before Buddhahood]] to become [[si-yuan-four-vows-bodhisattva-practice-yue-ming-jing|bodhisattva]] Vasumitra.

### The 84,000 Golden Monkeys

The Buddha further explains the karmic origin of the 84,000 golden monkeys present at the assembly. During the time of Buddha Krakucchanda, 84,000 bhikshunis at two kingdoms violated the Eight Respectful Practices (八敬法). When a virtuous arhat bhikshuni tried to correct them, they reviled her with insults ("old monkey"). Due to this karmic weight, they were reborn as animals for thousands of lifetimes, including as monkeys—though their golden color indicated residual meritorious potential from past offerings to the virtuous arhat.

King Bimbisara himself is revealed as one who had made offerings to these bhikshunis, and he attains [[initial-awakening|initial awakening]] upon hearing the Dharma.

### Doctrinal Significance

The sutra emphasizes several key Buddhist teachings:

1. **Power of Ethical Precepts**: Even a single sincere taking of the Five Precepts can liberate a being from animal rebirth and set them on the path to [[bodhisattva-path|bodhisattva enlightenment]].

2. **[[shan-e-bao-ying-karmic-retribution|Karmic Law]]**: The verse states: "Karma is like a contract that must be repaid; it follows like a shadow, never departing." Good karma leads to heavenly rebirth and eventual buddhahood, while evil karma results in the eighteen hells and animal rebirths.

3. **The [[bodhisattva-path|Bodhisattva Path]]**: Vasumitra's prediction as future Buddha demonstrates that beings from any origin—including animals—can achieve supreme enlightenment through accumulated merit and steadfast practice.

4. **[[three-poisons-greed-hatred-delusion-buddhist-teaching|Confession and Purification]]**: The sutra details how the arhat guided the monkey through confession of past misdeeds, enabling spiritual transformation.

### The Future Buddha

The sutra concludes with the Buddha predicting that after an enormous number of kalpas (劫), the 84,000 golden monkeys will simultaneously become Buddhas, all named "Universal Golden Radiance King Buddha" (普金光明王如來), heralding an extraordinary event in Buddhist cosmology.

## 中文

《師子月佛本生經》為佛教本生經典，屬於「遺教三錄」之一，收於《大正藏》第三冊。經文通過猴王受持五戒之故事，闡明[[shan-e-bao-ying-karmic-retribution|因果報應]]之理、持戒功德之力，以及一切眾生皆可成佛之教義。

### 經文結構與內容

本品結構可分為三大部分：

**一、緣起敘事**：佛陀住王舍城迦蘭陀竹園，弟子中有婆須蜜多比丘，以猿猴般之行為著称——攀樹上下、聲如猨猴、捉鈴作戲。此舉引起頻婆娑羅王之不悅，遣使詢問。佛陀應王之請，廣說本生因緣。

**二、本生故事**：往昔無量億劫之前，有獼猴至羅漢所，見羅漢坐禪入定，遂取袈裟繞行。羅漢憐愍此畜生善心，授以三歸五戒。獼猴發願持不殺戒，命終生於兜率天，值遇補處菩薩，聞說無上道心。此後經歷無數劫，親近承事無量諸佛，修諸梵行，具足六波羅蜜，當於彌勒佛後成佛，號「師子月如來」。

**三、獼猴集團之業報**：八萬四千金色獼猴，乃拘樓秦佛時犯戒比丘尼之餘報。彼女等謗毀羅漢比丘尼，由謗賢聖故，墮地獄八萬四千劫，餓鬼五百身，畜生二千五百身，終為獼猴。頻婆娑羅王前世曾供養彼等，故今得聞法開悟。

### 持戒功德之闡釋

經文詳述持戒之功德：

> 「戒為甘露藥，服者不老死，戒德可恃怙，福報常隨己。持戒得安隱，生處無患難，亦當見諸佛，受法得解脫。」

又云：

> 「持戒生天梯，破戒為鑊湯。我見持戒人，光明莊嚴身。」

此段文字充分體現佛教「諸惡莫作，眾善奉行」之基本精神，以及持戒解脫之修道次第。

### 業力因果之要義

經中羅漢偈言闡明因果報應之理：

> 「業能莊嚴身，處處隨趣趣，不失法如券，業如負財人。汝今生天上，由於五戒業，前身落獼猴，從於犯戒性。」

此偈說明：
1. 業力不失，如契約般必然報償
2. 持戒生天為解脫之階梯
3. 犯戒墮落為地獄之因
4. 犯戒與持戒之差異，決定來世苦樂

### 末世預言

經末明言：八萬四千金色獼猴，於將來過百萬億那由他阿僧祇恒河沙劫之後，同一劫中次第成佛，皆號「普金光明王如來」。此說顯示大乘佛教對一切眾生皆可成佛之信念。

## 日本語

『師子月佛本生経』は、猴子（猿）が五戒を受けて仏道を歩み、最終的に師子月如来となるまでの過去生を描く、本生譚（ジャataka）文学の傑作である。

### 経典の構成

本経は以下の三つの主要部分から構成される：

**第一部分：序説（縁起）**——ブッダが王舎城の竹林精舎に千二百五十人の比丘と共にいる場面から始まる。婆須蜜多（Vasumitra）という比丘が木に登り、猿のように振る舞う姿が頻婆娑羅王の批判を招く。

**第二部分：本生譚**——遠い昔、火天如来（然燈仏）の時代に、羅漢（阿羅漢）の修行者を見つけた猿が、袈裟を着けて香を焚く様子が語られる。羅漢は慈悲に基づき、この猴子に三帰依（帰依仏・帰依法・帰依僧）と五戒を授けた。猿は不殺生戒を発願し、高木から落ちて命を落とす。安那我漢の加持により、兜率天に生まれた後、無数の劫を経て諸仏に仕え、最終的に弥勒菩薩の次に仏となる。

**第三部分：八十四千匹の金色猿の原因**——拘樓秦仏の時代の八万四千人の比丘尼が戒律を破り、羅漢尼を侮辱した罪により、地獄・餓鬼・畜生道を転々と漂流。八万四千劫の地獄の果てに猿として金色で生まれた。王と婆須蜜多の因縁も明らかにされる。

### 戒律の力

経中の偈頌は戒律の重要性を強調する：

> 「戒は甘露の薬なり、服する者は老死不受理、
> 戒徳は恃む可く、福報は常に己に従う」

五戒、特に不殺生戒の効果は、畜生から天上への転生という劇的な変化をもたらし、それは解脱への道標となる。

### 因果応報の法則

羅漢の言葉は因果応報の本質を明示する：

> 「業は身を荘厳し、処処に趣趣と随い、
> 法を失わず券の如く、業は財を負担する人の如し」

善業は天上への道を開き、悪業は地獄への道を示す。この経典は、単一の善行さえ完全な解脱への転換点となり得ることを証している。
