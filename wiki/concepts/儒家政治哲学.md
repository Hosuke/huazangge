## English

Overview: Confucian political philosophy (儒家政治哲学) is the branch of Confucian thought that applies the moral principles of ren (benevolence), li (ritual propriety), and junzi (noble or exemplary person) to the exercise of political authority. It posits that the legitimacy of government rests on the moral virtue of the ruler and on the cultivation of a harmonious, virtuous society rather than on coercive power or the pursuit of material profit.

Core Principles

- **Benevolent Governance (仁政)**: The ruler must embody ren, treating the people with compassion and paternal care.
- **Virtue over Profit**: Political order is stable only when guided by righteousness (yi) and moral duty, not by naked self‑interest.
- **Moral Self‑Cultivation**: Both rulers and subjects are expected to practice self‑reflection, learning, and the refinement of character, which forms the foundation of good governance.
- **The Ideal of the Junzi**: The exemplary person serves as a model; in politics this means a ruler who rules by moral example, not by force.
- **Mandate of Heaven (天命) and the Unity of Tianxia**: Political authority is seen as a Heavenly trust that can be revoked if the ruler fails to uphold moral standards; the goal is the pacification and well‑being of all under heaven.
- **Human Harmony (人和)**: A well‑ordered state requires the harmonious cooperation of social roles and mutual respect among ruler, officials, and the populace.

Historical Development

- **Confucius (551–479 BC)**: Articulated the ideal of “rule by moral example” (德治) and the importance of li in maintaining social order.
- **Mencius (372–289 BC)**: Expanded the doctrine of innate moral nature (性善) and argued that a ruler who governs for the benefit of the people (“王天下”) will gain the “Mandate of Heaven”. He famously contrasted “义” (righteousness) with “利” (profit) in the “righteousness‑profit debate”.
- **Xunzi (c. 310–c. 237 BC)**: While more realist, his emphasis on li and ritual helped shape the later Confucian statecraft.
- **Han Dynasty (206 BC–220 AD)**: Confucianism became the official ideology; Dong Zhongshu’s “独尊儒术” integrated Confucian moral teachings with imperial administration.
- **Song‑Ming Neo‑Confucianism (理学)**: Cheng Yi, Zhu Xi, Wang Yangming re‑interpreted the political implications of Confucian ethics, emphasizing the unity of self‑cultivation and governance.
- **Modern Reinterpretations**: Reformist thinkers such as Kang Youwei and Liang Qichao re‑imagined Confucian political ideals within republican and constitutional frameworks.

Key Texts and Figures

- **Analects (论语)**: Contains Confucius’s teachings on governance, virtue, and the exemplary ruler.
- **Mencius (孟子)**: Presents the debates on benevolent rule, the Mandate of Heaven, and the “righteousness‑profit” dichotomy.
- **Xunzi (荀子)**: Discusses li, moral education, and the practical aspects of statecraft.
- **Zhu Xi’s “Four Books” (四书)**: Became the standard curriculum for civil service examinations, embedding Confucian political thought in the imperial bureaucracy.
- **Dong Zhongshu, Cheng Yi, Zhu Xi, Wang Yangming**: Key interpreters who shaped the evolution of the political dimension of Confucianism.

Legacy and Influence

Confucian political philosophy has shaped East Asian statecraft for over two millennia. Its emphasis on moral leadership contributed to the idea of the “civilizing mission” of the state, the meritocratic bureaucracy (科举), and the notion that governance should be judged by the well‑being of the people. In contemporary debates, Confucian ideas are invoked in discussions of democratic governance, human rights, and the ethics of leadership.

## 中文

### 概述

儒家政治哲学是儒家思想中关注政治秩序、统治合法性与治理方式的分支。它强调统治者的道德品质、仁爱之心以及社会和谐是政治稳固的根本，主张以德治国、以礼化民、以义制利。

### 核心理念

- **仁政**：君主应以仁爱之心对待百姓，实行“仁政”，以民为本。
- **德治**：政治合法性源于统治者的道德示范，强调“君子以德服人”。
- **义利之辩**：在《孟子》中，孟子明确反对以利益为中心的政治，主张“义”高于“利”，认为以利为本的政治必然导致社会道德瓦解。
- **天命与天下**：政治权力被视为天命的托付，若君主失德，天命可被收回；理想是实现“天下为公”的大同社会。
- **人和**：社会各阶层各司其职、相互尊重，形成积极的社会秩序，是仁政实现的前提。

### 历史发展

1. **孔子（公元前551‑前479）**：提出“德治”“礼治”概念，主张以道德感化来治理国家。  
2. **孟子（公元前372‑前289）**：深化性善论，提出“仁政”“王者兴”“五百年必有王者兴”之说，强调君主要以民利为先。  
3. **荀子（公元前310‑前237）**：虽然对人性的看法较现实，却仍坚持礼、义在政治中的重要性。
