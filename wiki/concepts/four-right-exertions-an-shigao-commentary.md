## English

The Four Right Exertions (四意斷, *caturaḥ-saṃbhāra*), also translated as the Four Right Efforts or Four Exertions of the Mind, constitute a crucial component of the [[thirty-seven-factors-enlightenment|三十七品]] (Thirty-Seven Factors of Enlightenment) in Buddhist practice. The Han Dynasty commentary attributed to An Shigao provides an early Chinese exposition of these practices, which form the cognitive and behavioral foundation for the cultivation of the Noble Eightfold Path.

### Definition and Purpose

The Four Right Exertions are defined as the deliberate efforts of the mind to: (1) prevent unwholesome mental states from arising, (2) abandon unwholesome states that have already arisen, (3) develop wholesome states that have not yet arisen, and (4) maintain and perfect wholesome states that have already arisen. These exertions are understood as the application of will (*prahāṇa*) in the service of spiritual development.

### The Four Exertions in Detail

#### First Exertion: Preventing Unwholesome States

The commentary states that the practitioner who encounters unwholesome dharmas (非法)—the twelve links of dependent origination leading to renewed existence—should apply skillful means to prevent their arising. The text emphasizes that evil roots, deeply established in the mindstream, pose a danger of rebirth if left unchecked. Therefore, one must preemptively generate the power of the path, advance virtue, and guard the path without laxity.

#### Second Exertion: Abandoning Arisen Unwholesome States

For unwholesome states that have already arisen, the practitioner should generate the desire for pure dharmas (清淨法)—specifically, the practices of *śamatha* (止) and *vipaśyanā* (觀). Through stopping the wicked and contemplating the four impermanences (*四非常*), the mind becomes purified. The practitioner must diligently restrain the six sense faculties, abandon all unwholesome thoughts, and advance toward the roots of awakening and the strength of faith.

#### Third Exertion: Developing Unmanifest Wholesome States

For wholesome states not yet arisen, the practitioner should arouse the mind and apply skillful means to bring them into being. This involves energizing the mind, restraining the sense faculties, and abandoning unwholesome thoughts. The commentary emphasizes that even when wholesome qualities have not yet appeared, they should be actively cultivated.

#### Fourth Exertion: Maintaining Wholesome States

For wholesome states already arisen, the practitioner should ensure they are not lost and do not diminish. The text states: "Stop, restrain the purified [states]; do not forget them, and do not allow them to decrease." The practitioner should advance virtue toward the limitless intention, allowing the path to reach fulfillment. This exertion involves continually generating skillful means, energizing the mind, restraining the senses, and abandoning unwholesome thoughts.

### Integration with the Four Foundations of Mindfulness

The commentary explicitly connects the Four Right Exertions with the [[four-foundations-mindfulness-an-shigao-commentary|四意止]] (Four Foundations of Mindfulness). The exertions provide the volitional and behavioral support for the meditative investigations of the foundations. Without right effort, mindfulness lacks the active component necessary for transformation; without mindfulness, effort lacks the clear comprehension necessary for wisdom.

### Significance in Buddhist Ethics and Practice

The Four Right Exertions represent the practical application of Buddhist ethical principles to the domain of mental cultivation. They transform abstract moral understanding into concrete behavioral strategies. The practitioner's mind becomes the battlefield where unwholesome and wholesome states contend, with right effort serving as the generalship that ensures victory for the wholesome.

## 中文

四意斷，又稱四正斷、四正勤，為三十七品之一，乃佛教修行者斷除惡法、增長善法之根本功夫。東漢安世高譯注之《陰持入經》對此有詳盡闡釋，反映了早期漢譯佛教對心靈修養之理解。

### 定義與目的

四意斷者，謂修行者於心念上有四種正確之努力：一者未生之惡法，令不生；二者已生之惡法，令斷滅；三者未生之善法，令得生；四者已生之善法，令不忘失、不令減少。此四正斷為聖道之精進方便，乃對治身心懈怠、增長覺悟之力用。

### 四正斷詳解

#### 第一斷意

《陰持入經》雲：「或見比丘，有未生弊惡意法，發方便令不生，勸意不捨方便。」註釋者解釋，此處「法」指十二因緣生死法。勸邪根深廣，懼其復生，當豫發道力進意德，守道無懈怠。行者遇非法之法，懼其復生，當預先發道力以防範之。

#### 第二斷意

經雲：「已生弊惡意，發清淨法欲斷，勸意求方便，精進攝制意，捨散惡意，是為二斷意。」清淨法者，止觀也。制止邪、念斷諸欲根、觀四非常，謂之淨法。行者當精其心、進其志，攝制六情、舍眾欲，散諸惡念，進就根力。

#### 第三斷意

經雲：「未生清淨法，勸意發方便令生，精進攝制意，捨散惡意，是為三斷意。」行者於清淨善法未生之時，當發心令生。止，制止。淨行情，無忘之，勿令減也。

#### 第四斷意

經雲：「已生清淨法，令止不忘、令不減。精進攝制意，捨散惡意，是為四意正斷。」啻，多也。足，滿也。行者當進德趣就無盡之意，令道滿足。

### 與四意止之關係

四正斷與[[four-foundations-mindfulness-an-shigao-commentary|四意止]]密切相關。四意止為觀察之功夫，四正斷為實踐之功夫。行者修四意止以觀察身受心法，同時修四正斷以防止惡法生起、斷滅已生惡法、令善法出生、令善法不退。二者相輔相成，共同成就解脫之道。

## 日本語

四意断（しいだん）は、四正断または四正勤とも呼ばれ、{{wp:三十七道品|三十七品}}の一つである。漢代の安世高の註釈において詳しく説明されており、仏教実践における倫理的・瞑想的基盤を構成している。

### 定義と目的

四意断とは、修行者の心が四つの正しい努力を指す：
1. 未生不善法を未生せしめざること
2. 已生不善法、既に生じた不善法を除去すること
3. 未生善法、未だ生起していない善法を起こさせること
4. 已生善法、既に生じた善法を不忘失にし、減少させないこと

これらの努力は精神発達のための意志（*{{wp:prahāṇa}}）の適用として理解される。

### 四つの努力の詳細

#### 第一の努力：不善法の発生予防

経典は「未生弊悪意法、方便を発して令生ぜず」と述べる。註釈者は「法」とは十二縁起の生死法を指すと言う。悪しき根源は深く心に残り、放置すれば再生の危険があるため、道力の予備的発生が必要である。

#### 第二の努力：生起した不善法の除去

経典は「已生弊悪意、清楚法を発して断ぜん」と述べる。{{wp:止観|止観}}の実践を通じて、邪悪を制止し、四無常を観察し、心を浄化する。修行者は六根を抑制し、すべての不善想念を捨て、根力と信力の向上に進む。

#### 第三の努力：善法の開発

経典は「未生清楚法、意を勸め方便を発して令生ぜしむ」と述べる。善法がまだ現れていない時も積極的に培養すべきであり、心を励まし、技術的手段を適用してそれらを実現する。

#### 第四の努力：善法の維持

経典は「已生清楚法、令止不忘、令不减」と述べる。善法が減少しないよう忘れず、守ることが重要であり、道が成就に至るまで徳を進んで追求する。
