## English

The Bodhisattva path is a fundamental concept in Mahayana Buddhism, representing the commitment to achieving enlightenment not only for oneself but also for all sentient beings. Bodhisattvas are individuals who, motivated by great compassion and wisdom, seek to liberate others from the cycle of birth and death (samsara) before attaining Buddhahood themselves.

Central to this path is the practice of the six perfections (paramitas), which include generosity (dāna), moral discipline (śīla), patience (kṣānti), effort (vīrya), concentration (samādhi), and wisdom (prajñā). Through the cultivation of these virtues, a Bodhisattva develops the qualities necessary to assist others on their spiritual journeys.

The aspiration to become a Bodhisattva is often expressed through formal vows, acknowledging the intent to support all living beings in overcoming suffering and attaining enlightenment. This path contrasts with the more individualistic approach found in some other Buddhist traditions, reflecting the foundational Mahayana principle of interdependence.

## 中文

菩萨道是大乘佛教的一个基本概念，代表着不仅为了自己而是为了所有有情众生实现开悟的承诺。菩萨是那些因伟大的慈悲和智慧而促使自己在获得菩提之前，努力解救他人脱离生死轮回的人。

这一道路的核心是六波罗蜜的实践，包括布施（dāna）、戒律（śīla）、忍耐（kṣānti）、精进（vīrya）、禅定（samādhi）和智慧（prajñā）。通过培养这些美德，菩萨发展出帮助他人精神修行所需的品质。

成为菩萨的志向通常通过正式的誓言来表达，承认帮助所有众生克服痛苦、获得开悟的意图。这条路径与其他一些佛教传统中更个体化的方式形成对比，反映出大乘佛教基础的相互依存原则。

## English

Mahayana Buddhism is recognized as one of the two primary branches of Buddhism, alongside Theravada Buddhism. It encompasses a rich variety of teachings and practices that emphasize the Bodhisattva ideal, where individuals aspire to attain Buddhahood not just for themselves, but for all sentient beings. This tradition introduces a broader understanding of what it means to be enlightened, suggesting that one should assist others on their path to liberation.

Key texts in Mahayana Buddhism, such as the "Sad Flower Sutra," focus on the interconnectedness of all beings and the necessity for compassion in the pursuit of wisdom. It promotes the practice of the six perfections (paramitas), which are essential virtues for a Bodhisattva's journey.

Mahayana Buddhism also embraces various philosophical teachings, including the concept of emptiness (śūnyatā) and the nature of reality, emphasizing the importance of understanding these principles to aid in the quest for enlightenment.

## 中文

大乘佛教是佛教的两个主要支派之一，与小乘佛教相对。它包含了丰富的教义和实践，强调菩萨理想，个体不仅追求自我成佛，还要为所有有情众生获得解脱而努力。这一传统对开悟的理解更加广泛，暗示个体应帮助他人寻找解脱之路。

大乘佛教的关键经典，如《悲華經》，关注所有众生的相互联系和在追求智慧过程中对慈悲的必要性。它提倡六波罗蜜的实践，作为菩萨修行的基本美德。

大乘佛教还接受了各种哲学教义，包括空性（śūnyatā）和现实的本质，强调理解这些原则对于帮助追求开悟的重要性。

また、大乗仏教は空（śūnyatā）や現実の本質に関するさまざまな哲学的教えも受け入れ、悟りを追求するためにこれらの原則の理解の重要性を強調しています。
