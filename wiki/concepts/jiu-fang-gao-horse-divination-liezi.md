## English

The Liezi contains numerous parables illustrating the gap between superficial appearance and essential truth. One of the most instructive is the account of **Jiu Fang Gao** (九方皋), a horse diviner whose approach to evaluation demonstrates profound wisdom about the nature of perception and judgment.

Duke Mu of Qin (秦穆公) asked Bo Le (伯樂), the renowned evaluator of horses, whether any of his descendants possessed the ability to identify fine horses. Bo Le replied that while his own sons could evaluate ordinary excellent horses based on their physical form and bone structure, they lacked the capacity to recognize **天下之馬** (horses of the realm). However, Bo Le recommended Jiu Fang Gao, a man who shared his profession of carrying firewood and grass, declaring him in no way inferior to himself.

Duke Mu summoned Jiu Fang Gao, who departed to find such a horse. Three months later, he returned reporting that he had located one at the Shaqiu (沙丘). When asked what kind of horse it was, Jiu Fang Gao replied simply: "A yellow mare." When others were sent to retrieve it, they found a **black stallion** (牡而驪). Duke Mu was displeased, complaining to Bo Le that the man sent to find horses could not even distinguish color or sex—how then could he know a horse?

Bo Le sighed deeply, recognizing in this failure a mark of supreme ability: "This is precisely why he is worth ten thousand of me and more. What Gao observes is the **天機** (heavenly mechanism)—the essential vitality of the horse. He grasps what is subtle and forgets what is coarse; attends to the inner nature and disregards the outer form. He sees what should be seen and does not see what need not be seen. He looks at what deserves attention and ignores what does not. The one who truly understands horse divination sees something more valuable than the horse itself."

When the horse arrived, it proved indeed to be a horse of the realm—a horse of supreme quality.

**Interpretation:**

This parable demonstrates a fundamental principle in classical Chinese thought: the distinction between **精** (essence) and **麤** (coarseness), between inner truth and outer appearance. Jiu Fang Gao's method represents a perfected form of evaluation that transcends ordinary perception. While ordinary evaluators judge by visible characteristics—color, sex, physical form—the master perceives the **內** (inner) and forgets the **外** (outer), grasps the essential vitality that constitutes true worth.

This teaching connects to broader themes in the Liezi and Daoist philosophy regarding the limitation of sensory perception and the need to cultivate deeper modes of understanding. The story also appears in the Zhuangzi with variations, indicating its importance across Warring States philosophical literature.

The narrative also contains implicit political-philosophical dimensions: just as the true horse of the realm cannot be identified by superficial examination, the true minister or worthy cannot be recognized by ordinary criteria. Rulers who rely solely on external appearances—whether in selecting officials or evaluating policies—will miss those of genuine value.

## 中文

《列子·說符篇》載有九方皋相馬之事，為古典中國哲學中關於本質與現象關係之重要寓言。秦穆公問伯樂曰：「子之年長矣，子姓有可使求馬者乎？」伯樂對曰：「良馬可形容筋骨相也。天下之馬者，若滅若沒，若亡若失，若此者絕塵弭轍。臣之子皆下才也，可告以良馬，不可告以天下之馬也。臣有所與共擔纍薪菜者，有九方皋，此其於馬非臣之下也。請見之。」

穆公見之，使行求馬。三月而反報曰：「已得之矣，在沙丘。」穆公曰：「何馬也？」對曰：「牝而黃。」使人往取之，牡而驪。穆公不說，召伯樂而謂之曰：「敗矣，子所使求馬者！色物、牝牡尚弗能知，又何馬之能知也？」

伯樂喟然太息曰：「一至於此乎！是乃其所以千萬臣而無數者也。若皋之所觀天機也，得其精而忘其麤，在其內而忘其外；見其所見，不見其所不見；視其所視，而遺其所不視。若皋之相者，乃有貴乎馬者也。」馬至，果天下之馬也。

**哲學意涵：**

此寓言闡發「得意忘言」之理，與《莊子》「得魚忘筌」之義相發明。九方皋之所以能得「天機」，在於其能「得其精而忘其麤，在其內而忘其外」。此「精」與「內」，指事物之本質與生機；「麤」與「外」，指形色、毛皮等外在表徵。

此理可推而至於治國用人之道。魏晉名士品評人物，貴能觀人之神韻而不拘形貌；唐代選拔賢才，亦強調「內舉不避親，外舉不避仇」。九方皋之法，正可為知人善任之範式。

## 日本語

『列子』說符篇に収められた九方皋の相馬の話は、本質と現象の関係について語る重要な寓言である。秦の穆公が伯楽に子孫に千里馬を見分ける能力がある者を求めたとき、伯楽は自分の息子たちは良馬の骨相は判別できるが天下の馬を見分ける力はないと答えた。しかし、共に薪を運んだ九方皋は自分にも劣らない人物だと推薦した。

穆公が九方皋を呼び、三ヶ月で沙丘で馬を見つけたと報告させた。「牝馬で黄色だ」と答えた九方皋だったが、馬を取り寄せると「牡馬で黒」だった。穆公は色をなして伯楽を責めた。伯楽は深く嘆息し、九方皋の卓越性を称えた：「皋の観るは天機なり是其の千万の臣にして数えることなき者也。精を得て麤を忘れ、内を得て外を忘れる」——九方皋は本質的活力を捉え、外見的特徴はさて置く、真の相馬師だという。

**解釈：**

この寓言は「精」と「麤」、「内」と「外」の区別を示し、中国古典哲学における本質直観の理論を例証する。九方皋の方法は通常の知覚を超越し、真の価値を見抜く能力を表象する。治国の面では、表面的特徴のみで人材を評価する者の限界を警告する教訓となる。
