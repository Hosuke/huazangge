## English

**Shamatha and Vipashyana (止觀)** is a combined meditative practice in Buddhism that integrates the calming of the mind (止, *zhǐ*, *shamatha*) with the clear insight into the nature of phenomena (觀, *guān*, *vipashyana*). It is regarded as the core method for achieving liberation and is especially emphasized in the Chinese, Japanese, Korean, and Tibetan traditions.

### Definition

- **止 (shamatha)**: The practice of stilling, concentrating, and quieting the mind, leading to a state of mental stability and tranquility.
- **觀 (vipashyana)**: The practice of seeing-through, observing phenomena as they truly are—impermanent, empty of self, and interdependent.

Together, 止觀 denotes the inseparable pair of “stillness and insight” that together produce wisdom and liberation.

### Historical Development

- **Early Buddhist sources**: The term appears implicitly in the *Pāli Canon* where the Buddha teaches “calming (samatha) and insight (vipassanā)”. The *Mahāvīra* and *Saṃyukta Āgama* texts also describe this dual practice.
- **Chinese systematization**: In the 6th century, the Tiantai master Zhiyi (智顗) compiled the monumental work *Mohe Zhiguan* (摩訶止觀), which systematized the theory and method of 止觀 into a comprehensive meditation curriculum. This text became the cornerstone for later Chinese and Japanese meditation manuals.
- **Japanese adoption**: Japanese Zen and Nichiren schools adopted the term as **止觀 (shōkan)**, emphasizing both seated meditation (zazen) and insight practice.
- **Tibetan parallel**: The Tibetan tradition uses the native terms *shamatha* (shinay) and *vipashyana* (lhaktong), which are functionally identical to 止觀.

### Practice

1. **Preliminary**: Ethical conduct (sila) and correct posture.
2. **止 (shamatha)**: Focus on a single object (breath, a kasina, a mantra, or a visualized object). Cultivate one‑pointedness until mental fluctuations subside.
3. **轉 (transition)**: When a stable level of concentration is reached, shift the focus to the arising and passing of phenomena, observing their conditioned nature.
4. **觀 (vipashyana)**: Direct examination of the five aggregates, dependent origination, emptiness, and the non‑self nature of all dharmas.
5. **融合 (integration)**: The meditator “moves with” the two aspects, allowing calm and insight to operate simultaneously, a state sometimes described as “止觀雙運” (the twin activities of stillness and seeing).

### Role in Buddhist Teachings

- **Foundation for liberation**: The *Mahāparinirvāṇa Sūtra* and the *Yogācāra* treatises state that without 止觀, true wisdom (prajñā) cannot arise.
- **Link with the Twelve Links of Dependent Origination**: The practice of 止觀 is considered essential for directly witnessing the twelve nidānas (e.g., avidyā → saṃskāra → … → jarā‑maraṇa), thereby cutting the cycle of rebirth. The article on “twelve‑links‑dependent‑origination” explicitly notes that 止觀 meditation is the key to understanding the conditional chain.
- **Mahāyāna emphasis**: In Mahāyāna sutras such as the *Heart Sūtra* and *Lotus Sūtra*, the interpenetration of calm and insight is expressed as “止觀一體” (one body of stillness and seeing).

## 中文

**止觀**，又稱**止觀禪**，是佛教中將「止」（止息、寂靜，亦稱為止禪、止定）與「觀」（觀照、觀慧，亦稱為觀禪、觀慧）相結合的修行法門。它被視為獲得解脫的核心方法，在漢傳佛教、天台宗、禪宗以及日本、韓國等佛教傳統中佔據重要地位。

### 止與觀的含義

- **止（止禪）**：通過專注單一對象（呼吸、佛像、咒語等），使心念止息、達到安定、清淨的境界。
- **觀（觀禪）**：在止的基礎上，以智慧觀照諸法實相，認識無常、無我、空性及緣起法則。

止觀合稱，意為「止息與觀慧」的相互依存，二者共同生成般若智慧。

### 歷史沿革

- **原始佛教**：在《阿含經》及相應的《雜阿含》中已有「止」與「觀」的教導。佛陀教授「止觀」為培育正定的必經之路。
