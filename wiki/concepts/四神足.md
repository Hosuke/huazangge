### Overview
Four Bases of Supernatural Power (Sanskrit: ṛddhipāda; Pali: iddhipāda; Chinese: 四神足; Japanese: 四神足, Shishinzoku) are a set of four mental factors described in early Buddhist texts as the necessary conditions for attaining and manifesting supernatural abilities (iddhi). They are enumerated as:

- **Desire (Chanda)** – the wish to achieve the goal.
- **Effort (Viriya)** – the energy and perseverance applied.
- **Mind (Citta)** – the directed intention and mental unification.
- **Discrimination (Vimamsā)** – the investigative insight into the nature of phenomena.

These four factors are said to be the “feet” (pāda) on which the “supernatural power” stands, providing a psychological infrastructure for the development of higher meditative attainments (jhāna) and the manifestation of the six supernatural powers (abhijñā).

### Etymology and Terminology
- **Sanskrit**: ṛddhi (spiritual power, success) + pāda (basis, foot). The term literally means “the foot of spiritual power.”
- **Pali**: Iddhipāda – iddhi (miraculous power) + pāda (foot, basis). The same meaning.
- **Chinese**: 四神足 – 四 (four) + 神 (divine, spiritual) + 足 (foot, basis). Translates as “the four spiritual feet/bases.”
- **Japanese**: 四神足 – same characters; pronounced “Shishinzoku” in Japanese.

### Canonical Sources
1. **Pali Canon** – The “Iddhipada‑vibhanga Sutta” (SN 51.1) and the “Sāmaññaphala Sutta” (DN 2) list the four bases as: chanda, viriya, citta, vimamsā. They are described as prerequisites for the attainment of the “six higher knowledges” (chal‑abhijñā).
