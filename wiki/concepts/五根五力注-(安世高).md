## English

The **五根五力注** (Five Roots and Five Powers Commentary) is an early Chinese Buddhist exegetical work attributed to the Han‑dynasty translator **An Shigao** (安世高). Though no independent manuscript survives, its contents are recorded in later catalogues and quoted in subsequent commentaries.

### Content

The commentary explains the **five roots** (五根) and **five powers** (五力) that form a core set of spiritual faculties within the broader framework of the **thirty‑seven bodhipāramitās** (三十七品道品). The five roots are:

1. **Faith (信根)**
2. **Zeal (精進根)**
3. **Mindfulness (念根)**
4. **Concentration (定根)**
5. **Wisdom (慧根)**

Corresponding to each root is a **power** (力) that manifests when the root is fully cultivated. An Shigao’s exposition classifies these faculties into the threefold training of Buddhist practice:

- **Morality (戒) –** supported by the roots of faith and zeal.
- **Concentration (定) –** supported by the roots of mindfulness and concentration.
- **Wisdom (慧) –** supported
