## English

Mozi's "Banners and Flags" chapter presents a comprehensive color-coded identification system designed for urban siege defense. This system enabled defenders to rapidly identify personnel roles, material types, and resource levels during enemy attacks.

### Color-Coded Banner System

The chapter establishes a sophisticated visual communication framework:

| Material/Role | Banner Color | Purpose |
|---------------|--------------|---------|
| Wood | Green (蒼) | Construction materials |
| Fire | Red (赤) | Fire-related equipment |
| Firewood | Yellow (黃) | Fuel supplies |
| Stone | White (白) | Defensive stones |
| Water | Black (黑) | Water resources |
| Food | Mushroom (菌) | Provisions |
| Brave warriors | Warehouse Red (倉英) | Elite fighters |
| Combat soldiers | Tiger (虎) | Front-line troops |
| Multiple soldiers | Double Rabbit (雙兔) | Infantry units |
| Five-foot men | Child (童) | Adult male defenders |
| Women | Branch-tip (梯末) | Female defenders |
| Crossbows | Dog (狗) | Archery units |
| Halberds | 𦲵 (specific) | Infantry weapons |
| Sword and shield | Feather (羽) | Close combat units |
| Chariots | Dragon (龍) | Cavalry support |
| Cavalry | Bird (鳥) | Mounted units |

### Communication Protocol

When a banner not listed in the code appears, defenders use its actual physical form as identification. When the city wall raises a banner, the supply officials assess resource needs and distribute materials accordingly, then lower the banner to signal completion.

## English

Mozi's chapter on "Banners and Flags" (旗幟) presents a comprehensive military communication system essential for organized siege defense. This system integrates visual signals through colored banners with auditory signals via drums to coordinate responses across the entire defended city.

### Color-Coded Banner System

The foundation of Mozi's military communication rests on a sophisticated color-coding system where each hue represents a specific category of resources, personnel, or threat level:

- **蒼旗 (Cāng qí)** - Green/Blue banners: Made of wood, indicating wooden materials and wooden components
- **赤旗 (Chì qí)** - Red banners: Made of fire/ignition materials for counter-attack preparations
- **黃旗 (Huáng qí)** - Yellow banners: Indicating firewood and kindling supplies
- **白旗 (Bái qí)** - White banners: Representing stone materials for defensive barriers
- **黑旗 (Hēi qí)** - Black banners: Signifying water resources and water-related defenses
- **菌旗 (Jùn qí)** - Mushroom-shaped banners: Representing food supplies and provisions
- **倉英之旗 (Cāng yīng zhī qí)** - Amber-gold banners: Marking elite "death-defying warriors" (死士)
- **虎旗 (Hǔ qí)** - Tiger banners: Designating "contending warriors" (竟士) ready for combat
- **雙兔之旗 (Shuāng tù zhī qí)** - Double rabbit banners: Indicating multiple infantry units
- **童旗 (Tóng qí)** - Youth banners: Marking five-foot男子 (male) defenders
- **梯末之旗 (Tī mò zhī qí)** - Ladle-end banners: Designating female defenders
- **狗旗 (Gǒu qí)** - Dog banners: Indicating crossbow (弩) units
- **𦲵旗 (Qíng qí)** - Halberd banners: Marking戟 (ji) halberd units
- **羽旗 (Yǔ qí)** - Feather banners: Signifying sword and shield (劍盾) units
- **龍旗 (Lóng qí)** - Dragon banners: Representing chariot (車) units
- **鳥旗 (Niǎo qí)** - Bird banners: Marking cavalry (騎) units

The system includes a catch-all provision: "凡所求索，旗名不在書者，皆以其形名爲旗" (Anything not explicitly named should use a banner depicting its actual form). This ensures flexibility while maintaining visual clarity.

### Siege Defense Preparations

Beyond the signal system, Mozi details extensive material preparations required for effective siege defense:

The defensive stockpile must include:
- Stone (石有積)
- Firewood and kindling (樵薪有積)
- Rushes and thatch (菅茅有積)
- Reeds and rushes (萑葦有積)
- Wood (木有積)
- Charcoal (炭有積)
- Sand (沙有積)
- Pine and cypress (松柏有積)
- Wormwood and mugwort (蓬艾有積)
- Hemp and fat/oil (麻脂有積)
- Metal and iron (金鐵有積)
- Grain (粟米有積)
- Wells and kilns at designated locations (井竈有處)
- Heavy quality materials secured in place (重質有居)
- Five types of weapons each with their own banner (五兵各有旗)
- Distinct badges/identifiers for each rank (節各有辨)
- Clear legal codes with standardized weights and measures (法令各有貞，輕重分數各有請)
- Designated road supervisors with fixed routes (主慎道路者有經)

### Drum and Flag Coordination System

The heart of the signal system coordinates the response to enemy approach through an elaborate drum and flag protocol:

**Pavilion Officer (亭尉) Signal Standards:**
- Flagpole length: 2 zhàng 5 chǐ (approximately 8.3 meters)
- Banner silk: 1 zhàng 5 chǐ long (approximately 5 meters)
- Width: Half a standard span (廣半幅)

**Enemy Approach Response Protocol:**

| Enemy Position | Drum Beats | Flags Raised |
|----------------|------------|--------------|
| Outside the moat (前池外廉) | 3 drums | 1 flag |
| Around the moat water (水中周) | 4 drums | 2 flags |
| At the fence (藩) | 5 drums | 3 flags |
| At the outer wall (馮垣) | 6 drums | 4 flags |
| At the breastwork (女垣) | 7 drums | 5 flags |
| At the main wall (大城) | 8 drums | 6 flags |
| On the main wall, over half height (乘大城半以上) | Continuous鼓 (drum) | No change |

**Nighttime Protocol:**
Night attacks use fire signals instead of flags, following the same numerical progression.

**Retreat Protocol:**
When enemies withdraw, reverse the flags and drums according to the approach numbers, but without the drums.

### Command Structure and Unit Markings

The system establishes clear command hierarchy with body-position markings:

- **City Commander (城將)**: 50 feet authority, positioned centrally
- **Four Gate Commanders (四面四門將)**: 40 feet authority each
- **Secondary commanders**: 30 feet, 25 feet, 20 feet, 15 feet in descending order
- **Minimum height requirement**: No less than 45 feet

**Body Position Assignments:**
- Upper officers placed on the back; soldiers on the head
- Lower officers placed on the shoulder; soldiers on the head
- Left army on left shoulder; right army on right shoulder
- Central army on the chest
- Each position marked with one鼓 (drum)
- Central army: one鼓, three beats
- All drum-holding officers respond according to their rank
- Failure to respond appropriately results in execution (主者斬)

### Infrastructure and Path Management

The system includes logistical infrastructure:

- **Path width**: 30 steps between and along pathways
- **Wells**: Positioned at intervals along paths
- **Iron hooks (鐵𢑆)**: Installed for defensive purposes
- **Screening walls (屏)**: Erected 30 steps outside, 1 zhàng high
- **Latrines (民圂)**: Walls at least 12 feet high
- **Gates**: Required at all road intersections, guarded by two men each
- **Identification required**: No movement without信符 (credentials), violators executed

### Unit Identification and Incentive System

Combat units receive clear identification through the banner system:

- All defenders wear distinctive uniforms with identifying insignia
- Males and females distinguished by dress codes
- Units guarding defensive barriers (牲格) receive rewards based on performance
- Three successful sorties earn rewards
- Those defending receive banners marked with their names (署百戶邑若他人財物，建旗其署)
- This system creates accountability and recognition for valor

### Military Training Requirements

The system mandates ongoing military readiness:

- Defensive barrier (牲格) specifications: Inner width 25 steps, outer width 10 steps
- Length measured according to terrain (表以地形爲度)
- Soldiers trained in drills (勒卒中教)
- Rotating rest schedules for fatigued troops (前後左右，卒勞者更休之)

This comprehensive system demonstrates Mozi's sophisticated understanding of military logistics, communication, and human resource management during siege warfare.

上下相視：吏卒居背，士卒居頭；城下吏卒居肩，左軍於左肩，右軍於右肩，中軍居胷。各置一鼓，中軍一而三擊之，諸有鼓者以次應之。應而不應，不當應而應者，主者斬。

### 道路關隘管理

道廣三十步，夾階者各二井，置鐵𢑆。道外爲屏，三十步一圜，高一丈。民圂垣高十二尺以上。巷術周道必爲門，門二人守之，非有信符勿行，違令者斬。

### 賞罰激勵制度

城中吏卒民男女皆佩異衣章微，令可知知。守牲格者三出却敵，守以令召賜食，予大旗，署百戶邑若他人財物，建旗其署，令皆明白知之。

此制以旗幟明賞罰，以爵邑激人心，使守城之士知所勤惰。

### 訓練休整之制

牲格外廣二十五步，內廣十步，表以地形爲度。勒卒中教，解前後左右，卒勞者更休之。

訓練有法，休更有時，此所以養鋭氣而待敵也。

## English

The "Banners and Flags" (旗幟) chapter of the Mozi text presents a sophisticated military communication and identification system designed for defensive warfare. This system enabled defenders to coordinate resources, track enemy movements, and maintain organized resistance during siege operations through a standardized visual signaling apparatus.

### Color-Coded Resource Identification

The foundational element of the system assigned specific colors to different materials stockpiled for defense. Wood was marked with the *cang* (蒼, dark green-blue) flag; fire-related materials carried the *chi* (赤, vermillion red) flag; firewood and kindling used the *huang* (黃, yellow) flag; stones employed the *bai* (白, white) flag; water supplies featured the *hei* (黑, black) flag; and food provisions displayed the *jun* (菌, mushroom-shaped) flag. This chromatic coding allowed rapid identification of resource categories across the defensive positions.

### Military Unit Identification

Each military unit and personnel category received distinctive flags for battlefield recognition:

- **Dead soldiers** (死士): Cangying (倉英) flag, depicting the grain-shooting star
- **Elite warriors** (竟士): Tiger (虎) flag symbolizing ferocity
- **Multiple soldiers** (多卒): Double rabbit (雙兔) flag
- **Five-chi adult males** (五尺男子): Child (童) flag
- **Women**: Timo (梯末) flag
- **Crossbow units** (弩): Dog (狗) flag
- **Halberd units** (戟): Ji (𦲵) flag, a specialized halberd symbol
- **Sword and shield units** (劒盾): Feather (羽) flag
- **Chariot units** (車): Dragon (龍) flag
- **Cavalry units** (騎): Bird (鳥) flag

The text concludes with a crucial provision: any personnel or unit whose flag designation does not appear in the codified list should display a flag bearing their distinctive form or name.

### Enemy Approach Signaling System

The communication protocol established precise drum and flag signals corresponding to enemy penetration distances:

| Enemy Position | Drum Beats | Flags Displayed |
|----------------|------------|-----------------|
| Outside moat (前池外廉) | 3 | 1 |
| Surrounding water (水中周) | 4 | 2 |
| Outer fence (藩) | 5 | 3 |
