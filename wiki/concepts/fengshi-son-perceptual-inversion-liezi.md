## English

**The Son of Feng Clan with Perceptual Inversion** (逢氏之子迷罔) is a philosophical narrative from the "Liezi" text, specifically found in the "Chapter on King Mu of Zhou" (周穆王篇). This story presents a dramatic case of complete perceptual inversion, where a once-intelligent child loses the ability to correctly perceive sensory information, mistaking sweetness for bitterness, white for black, and right for wrong.

**The Narrative:**

The Feng clan of Qin had a son who was intelligent and talented as a child. However, upon reaching adulthood, he developed a mysterious affliction called "迷罔" (mí wǎng) — a state of confusion and perceptual inversion. His symptoms included:

- Hearing singing as crying
- Seeing white as black
- Smelling fragrance as decay
- Tasting sweetness as bitterness
- Considering wrong as right

In essence, every sensory input and moral judgment was inverted. The world as he perceived it was fundamentally different from the world as others perceived it. His perceptions of heaven and earth, the four directions, water and fire, cold and heat—all were completely reversed and confused.

**Yangzi's Consultation:**

A man named Yangzi (楊氏) advised the father: "The gentlemen of Lu are accomplished in many arts and techniques. Perhaps they can cure him. Why don't you go and seek their help?"
