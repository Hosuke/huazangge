## English

The "Seven Places and Three Contemplations Sutra" (七處三觀經) presents a practical ethical framework classifying people into four types based on their conduct regarding self-protection and protection of others. This classification, appearing in sections 8 and 9, serves as a meditation on moral conduct and spiritual development.

### The Four Types

**Type 1: Those Who Protect Themselves But Not Others (自護不護他)**
This type guards their own body, speech, and mind but does not concern themselves with the welfare of others. While maintaining personal virtue, they lack the compassionate dimension of broader ethical engagement.

**Type 2: Those Who Protect Others But Not Themselves (護他不自護)**
This type actively protects and cares for others while neglecting self-cultivation. They may engage in beneficial activities for others but fail to maintain proper ethical discipline in their own conduct.

**Type 3: Those Who Protect Neither Themselves Nor Others (俱不護)**
This type neither guards themselves nor protects others. The sutra describes this as "是最下賤人" (the most degraded person), representing complete moral failure in both personal and interpersonal dimensions.

**Type 4: Those Who Protect Both Themselves and Others (俱護)**
This type maintains proper discipline in body, speech, and mind while also actively caring for the welfare of others. The sutra identifies this as "最第一" (the most supreme), representing the ideal integration of self-cultivation and compassion.

### The Hierarchical Ordering

The sutra establishes a clear hierarchy:

1. **Highest**: Those who protect both self and others
2. **Higher**: Those who protect themselves but not others
3. **Lower**: Those who protect others but not themselves
4. **Lowest**: Those who protect neither

佛言：「是人不自護亦不護他人，是最下賤人；護他人不自護是勝上；若人自護不護他人是勝上；若自護亦護他是勝上。若如是人最第一。」

### The Teaching on Guarding (守)

The sutra elaborates on what it means to "guard" through the teaching on the "Four Types of Guarding" (四舍):

1. **舍舍** (Abandoning Guard) - Not accepting or receiving; maintaining distance from harmful influences and anger
2. **守舍** (Guarding Guard) - Protecting the sense doors (eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind) by not accepting unwholesome objects
3. **護舍** (Purifying Guard) - Contemplating the impurities of the body (corpse meditation) to maintain mindfulness
4. **行舍** (Practicing Guard) - Progressively advancing through discernment, distinguishing and separating from defilements

### The Analogy of the Covered House

The sutra uses a powerful metaphor:

「譬喻迦羅越！若樓若堂屋不覆，若使雨來，柞亦漬椽亦漬壁亦漬，已漬壁亦腐椽亦腐柞亦腐，譬如是。迦羅越！己意不守，身行亦不守，口行亦不守，念行亦不守，己意聲身不守便不善，死亦不善受亦不善處。」

"Just as, O householder, when a tower or hall has no roof, if rain comes, the beams rot, the rafters rot, the walls rot; when the walls rot, the rafters rot, the beams rot. So too, O householder, when one does not guard the mind, body conduct is unguarded, speech conduct is unguarded, mental conduct is unguarded. When mind, speech, and body are unguarded, they are not good. Death is not good, the result is not good, the destination is not good."

In contrast:

「若樓若堂屋上覆蓋，若便雨來，柞亦不漬椽亦不漬壁亦不漬，已不漬壁亦不腐椽亦不腐柞亦不腐，如是譬喻。」

"When the tower or hall has a roof, even if rain comes, the beams do not rot, the rafters do not rot, the walls do not rot."

### The Teaching on Guarding the Mind

佛從後說絕：「不守意者，邪疑故，亦睡眠故，魔便得自在。如是但當守意，若欲諦行但當見諦行，亦當知內出已，不墮睡眠，便得斷苦本。」

"Those who do not guard the mind, due to wrong view and sloth, give opportunity to Mara. Thus one should guard the mind. If one desires to walk the path truly, one should practice seeing the truth, should know the inner exit, should not fall into sloth, and will thereby cut off the root of suffering."

### Significance for Practice

This fourfold classification serves as both a diagnostic tool for practitioners to assess their spiritual development and a motivational framework encouraging progress toward the ideal of protecting both self and others. It integrates the personal discipline of self-guardianship with the broader ethical responsibility of caring for others, reflecting the Mahayana ideal of benefiting both self and other even in its early Chinese articulation.

## 中文

《七處三觀經》在第八和第九品中提出了四等人的倫理分類法，根據人們是否自護、是否護他來區分人的品德層次。這一分類法既是禪觀的對境，也是實踐修行的指南。

### 四等人的內涵

**第一種：自護不護他**

這種人能守護自己的身、口、意三業，卻不關心他人的福祉。他們雖然保持個人品德，卻缺乏更廣泛的慈悲倫理維度。

**第二種：護他不自護**

這種人積極護持他人，卻忽視自我修養。他們可能對他人行善，卻不能維持自身行為的倫理紀律。

**第三種：俱不護（自護護他皆無）**

這種人既不守護自己，也不護持他人。經文稱之為「是最下賤人」，代表個人修養與人際倫理兩方面的完全失敗。

**第四種：俱護（自護護他皆具）**

這種人既能保持身、口、意的清淨紀律，又能積極關懷他人的福祉。經文稱之為「最第一」，代表自我修養與慈悲兩方面的理想整合。

### 等級次第

經文確立了明確的層級結構：

一等：自護亦護他（最高）
二等：自護不護他
三等：護他不自護
四等：俱不護（最下）
