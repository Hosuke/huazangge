## English

### Overview
The **Four Noble Truths** (Pali: *Cattāri ariyasaccāni*; Sanskrit: *Āryasatya*; Chinese: 四聖諦; Japanese: 四聖諦) are the first and most fundamental teaching delivered by the Buddha after his enlightenment, traditionally known as the “first turning of the wheel of Dharma.” They form a concise diagnostic framework for understanding and addressing the human condition.

### The Four Truths

1. **Dukkha (Suffering)** – Existence is characterized by pain, frustration, and impermanence. Even moments of apparent pleasure are ultimately unsatisfactory because they are subject to change.
