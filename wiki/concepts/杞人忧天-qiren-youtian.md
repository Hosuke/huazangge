## English

**The Man Who Worried About Heaven Falling (杞人憂天)** is one of the most celebrated anecdotes in classical Chinese literature, preserved in the *Liezi*'s *Tianrui* chapter. The story serves as a philosophical lesson about the futility of worrying over matters beyond human control.

### The Original Story

The narrative describes a person from the state of Qi who constantly worried that the heavens might collapse and the earth might fall apart, leaving him with nowhere to live. This anxiety was so severe that he neglected eating and sleeping.

Another person, upon hearing of this worry, went to comfort him, explaining that the heavens are merely accumulated qi with no place devoid of qi, and that the sun, moon, and stars are merely luminous manifestations within this qi. Similarly, the earth is merely accumulated earth blocks filling the four directions, with no place lacking these blocks. One walks upon the earth constantly without concern, so why worry about its collapse?
