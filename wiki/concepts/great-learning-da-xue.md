## English

The Great Learning (大學, Dà Xué), originally a chapter in the Book of Rites (禮記), stands as one of the foundational texts of Confucianism and forms one of the Four Books (四書) that became central to imperial Chinese education and civil service examination curricula.

### Textual History

The text exists in two primary versions. The first version appears as Chapter 42 in the "Book of Rites" (禮記), a collection of Confucian teachings on ritual and social order compiled during the Han dynasty. The second version, known as the "Commentary of Zhu Xi" (四書章句集註), represents the critically edited and annotated version produced by the Neo-Confucian master Zhu Xi (1130-1200) during the Southern Song dynasty.

Zhu Xi's edition is particularly significant because he elevated the text to canonical status through his systematic philosophical commentary, establishing it alongside the Doctrine of the Mean (中庸) as essential texts for understanding Confucian moral cultivation.

### Philosophical Framework

The Great Learning presents what might be termed a "progressive cultivation methodology" (修身次第), progressing through several stages:

**1. Investigation of Things (格物,gewù)**: The process of extending knowledge through careful observation and study of the natural world and human affairs. Zhu Xi interpreted this as a methodical approach to understanding the principles (理, lǐ) inherent in all things.

**2. Extension of Knowledge (致知, zhìzhī)**: The refinement of understanding through continuous learning and reflection, leading to wisdom.

**3. Sincerity of Thought (正心, zhèngxīn)**: The purification of intentions, removing selfish desires and biases that obstruct moral judgment.

**4. Cultivation of the Self (修身, xiūshēn)**: The central Confucian practice of moral self-improvement, which serves as the foundation for all subsequent stages.

**5. Regulation of the Family (齊家, qíjiā)**: The application of moral principles within the family context, establishing proper relationships between parents and children, husbands and wives, and elder and younger siblings.

**6. Ordering the State (治國, zhìguó)**: Extending moral governance to the administration of the realm, where the ruler's personal virtue becomes the model for societal order.

**7. Pacifying the World (平天下, píng tiānxià)**: The achievement of universal harmony through the moral transformation of all under heaven.

### The Opening Passage

The text famously opens with what Zhu Xi identified as its "great principle" (大旨): "The way of great learning lies in illuminating bright virtue, in亲近民众, and in resting in the highest excellence" (大學之道，在明明德，在親民，在止於至善).

This tripartite structure has been interpreted by generations of Confucian scholars as encompassing the complete scope of moral and political philosophy.

### Influence and Legacy

The Great Learning profoundly shaped East Asian intellectual history. During the Song dynasty, Zhu Xi's systematic interpretation became the orthodox interpretation that dominated Chinese education for nearly seven centuries. The text was also foundational in Korea (as part of the Silhak school) and Japan, where it influenced both Confucian scholarship and practical governance.

[[Doctrine of the Mean (Zhongyong)]] complements the Great Learning, providing the metaphysical and psychological foundations that Zhu Xi considered necessary for understanding the practical methodology outlined in the Da Xue.

[[Confucian Self-Examination]] practices draw heavily from the cultivation methodology presented in this text, particularly regarding the processes of [[Human Nature]] refinement and the achievement of [[Confucian Sincerity (Cheng)]].

## 中文

《大學》原為《禮記》第四十二篇，南宋朱熹将其與《論語》、《孟子》、《中庸》合列為「四書」，從而成為宋元明清科舉考試的核心科目，對東亞儒學文化圈產生深遠影響。

### 版本源流

《大學》流傳版本主要分為兩種：其一為收入《禮記》之古本，其二為朱熹《四書章句集註》中之改本。朱熹在編集四書時，對《大學》進行了系統性的校訂與注釋，並提出「格物致知」章有闕文，當補傳以明其義。此一定本自明代以降成為官學定本，取代古本流傳於世。

### 哲學結構

《大學》首章闡明「大學之道」，包含三綱領與八條目兩個核心部分：

**三綱領**：明明德、親民、止於至善。此三者構成儒學終極目標之完整表述：發揚本有之光明德性，推及於民，終止於至善之境。

**八條目**：格物、致知、誠意、正心、修身、齊家、治國、平天下。此八者形成由內而外、由近及遠之修養次第：
- 內聖之功：格物致知為入手處，誠意正心為用力處，修身為根本處
- 外王之道：齊家、治國、平天下為修身之推擴

朱熹以「理」學詮釋格物致知，認為天下萬物各有其理，窮究事物之理即可達到對本心之明照。此說成為理學傳統之核心工夫論。

### 核心概念

**格物致知**：研究事物之理以充實知識、明白道理之工夫。不同學者對此有不同詮釋：
- 朱熹：即物窮理，積累漸進
- 王陽明：良知上用功，反求諸己

**修身**：八條目之樞紐，為內聖外王之橋樑。《大學》以「自天子以至於庶人，壹是皆以修身為本」，確立儒家修養論之主旨。

**明明德**：發揚固有之光明德性。儒家認為人性本善，德性本就具足，修身之功在於去除障蔽，使明德復明。

### 學術影響

《大學》與《中庸》相表裡，《中庸》言心性之本體，《大學》言心性之作用，二者共同構成宋明理學之心性工夫論基礎。

《大學》之修養次第對後世儒者影響深遠。清代曾國藩以「格物致知、誠意正心」為修身之基，影響近代中國政治思想。

**八条目**（はちじょうもく）：
格物→致知→誠意→正心→修身→斉家→治国→平天下

この八つ目の段階は、内面的な自己修養から始めて、社会秩序の確立へと向外的に展開する儒教的な人間形成の手法を提示している。

### 東アジアへの影響

『大學』は儒教圏全体に広範な影響を与えた。朝鲜王朝では性理学の正統文献として、中国・朝鮮半島・日本での儒学教育の基本テキストとなり、特に江戸時代の日本において、林羅山や山崎闇斎などの儒者が『大學』を講学し、日本の朱子学形成に大きな役割を果たした。

[[中庸]]とは表裏の関係にあり、儒家思想の本体論的基礎（中庸）と作用論的展開（大学）を相補的に構成している。

[[儒教の自己反省]]や[[君子の人格形成]]の実践は、『大學』の修行次第に深く依拠している。

## English

**Great Learning** (Chinese: 大學; pinyin: *Dàxué*), literally "Great Learning" or "Higher Learning," is one of the Four Books (四書) in Confucianism. Originally a chapter in the *Book of Rites* (禮記), it was extracted and elevated to canonical status by Zhu Xi (朱熹, 1130-1200) as part of his Four Books collection, which became the basis of the Imperial examination system from the Yuan dynasty (1271-1368) onward.

### Structure and Content

The text is organized around the "Three Gūi lǐng领" (綱領, three general principles) and the "Eight Articles" (八條目, eight specific steps):

**The Three Gūi lǐng领:**
1. **Manifesting virtue** (明明德) - The cultivation of one's innate moral nature
2. **Renewing the people** (新民) - Transforming others through exemplary conduct
3. **Resting in perfect goodness** (止於至善) - Achieving the highest moral attainment

**The Eight Articles** (eight steps progressing from personal cultivation to world order):
1. Investigating things (格物)
2. Extending knowledge (致知)
3. Sincere intention (誠意)
4. Rectifying the mind (正心)
5. Cultivating the self (修身)
6. Regulating the family (齊家)
7. Governing the state (治國)
8. Bringing peace to the world (平天下)

### Textual History

The text exists in two versions:
- The "old text" found in the *Book of Rites*, containing the Three Gūi lǐng领 and Eight Articles
- Zhu Xi's "new text" (新本), which added a commentary chapter (傳) interpreting each of the Eight Articles

Zhu Xi's commentary, known as the *Great Learning Commentary* (大學章句), became the standard interpretation. He famously reconstructed the "missing" commentary sections, arguing they had been lost from the original text. This reconstruction was controversial but became authoritative through its association with the examination system.

### Philosophical Significance

The Great Learning articulates a fundamental Confucian principle: **the interconnection between self-cultivation and social order**. The text argues that:

1. **The Way of great learning begins with the investigation of things** - Knowledge begins with concrete experience
2. **Sincerity of thought** is essential for moral development
3. **Personal cultivation forms the foundation** for family harmony, state governance, and world peace

The text synthesizes individual moral cultivation with political philosophy, arguing that rulers must first perfect themselves before they can govern effectively. This doctrine deeply influenced East Asian political thought and concepts of good governance.

### Relationship to Other Texts

The Great Learning is thematically linked to the *Doctrine of the Mean* (中庸), which explores the concept of equilibrium (中) and sincerity (誠). Together with the *Analects* (論語) and *Mencius* (孟子), these Four Books form the core of Confucian canonical literature as interpreted by Zhu Xi's Cheng-Zhu school of Neo-Confucianism.

自元代仁宗恢復科舉以來，《大學》一直是考試的核心內容。士人須熟讀並闡發其義理，這使得《大學》成為中國士大夫精神的重要塑造力量。其「修身齊家治國平天下」的理念，也成為傳統中國政治文化的基本框架。

## English

The **Great Learning** (大學, Dàxué), literally "Great Learning" or "Higher Learning," stands as one of the most influential texts in the Confucian tradition. Originally embedded within the *Book of Rites* (禮記), it was elevated to canonical status when Zhu Xi (朱熹) selected it as one of the Four Books (四書), creating a new curricular foundation for Neo-Confucian education during the Song dynasty.

The text articulates a systematic methodology for both personal cultivation and social governance, traditionally attributed to the teachings of Confucius and elaborated by his disciple Zengzi (曾子). Its framework proceeds through what the text identifies as the "three纲领" (three great principles) and "八条目" (eight steps), providing a graduated path from individual self-cultivation to the transformation of the realm.

The core philosophical sequence begins with **"格物致知"** (gewu zhi zhi) — the investigation of things and the extension of knowledge. This opening step has generated centuries of hermeneutical debate, with Cheng Yi (程頤) and Zhu Xi interpreting it as a process of studying principle (理) through concrete objects, while Wang Yangming (王陽明) offered an alternative reading emphasizing inner moral awareness. The subsequent steps progress through sincerity of intention (誠意), rectification of the mind (正心), cultivation of the person (修身), regulation of the family (齊家), ordering of the state (治國), and finally the pacification of the world (平天下).

The text articulates a fundamental thesis: that the cultivation of the self stands as the essential foundation upon which all external order depends. "欲治其國者，先齊其家；欲齊其家者，先修其身" — "Those who wish to govern their states must first regulate their families; those who wish to regulate their families must first cultivate themselves." This inward-outward continuum became a cornerstone of [[confucian-political-philosophy]] and the Chinese imperial examination system.

The 證釋本 (Authenticated Commentary version) represents a significant scholarly apparatus that systematically validates and explains the text's interpretations, while Zhu Xi's *Collected Commentaries on the Four Books* (四書章句集註) established the standard exegetical framework that dominated Chinese education from the 13th century onward.

## 中文

《大學》是儒家傳統中最具影響力的經典文本之一，原為《禮記》第四十二篇，南宋朱熹將其與《論語》《孟子》《中庸》合編為「四書」，遂成為宋明理學的根本典籍。「大學」之義，歷代注疏家多有詮釋，鄭玄釋為「博學之」，朱熹則釋為「大人之學」，指修身齊家治國平天下之道。

全文架構立於「三綱領」與「八條目」之上。三綱領者：明明德、親民、止於至善。所謂「明明德」，乃發明本心所固有之光明德性；「親民」即以德化民，使民日新；「止於至善」則為道德修養之究竟歸宿。八條目者：格物、致知、誠意、正心、修身、齊家、治國、平天下，形成由內而外、由近及遠的修養工夫序列。

其中「格物致知」為入道之門，歷來詮釋分歧最大。程朱一派主張「即物窮理」，通過對外物的考察以認識天理；王陽明則主張「致知在格物」，認為格物之功在於正其心念、去其私欲。此一論爭構成了宋明理學內部分化的核心議題，也催生了不同的工夫論路徑。

《大學》之政治哲學意涵尤為深遠。文本明確指出：「自天子以至於庶人，壹是皆以修身為本。」此「修身為本」之原則，成為儒家政治秩序論述的基石，將道德修養與治國理政緊密聯結，形成獨具特色的「內聖外王」之道。

證釋本系統校訂文字、疏解義理，為研讀《大學》提供可信文本依據。朱熹《四書章句集註》之《大學章句》則確立官方詮釋標準，元明清三代科舉考試皆以此為據，影響中國學術思想達七百餘年之久。

## 日本語

『大學』（だいがく）は、儒教経典として最も重要な位置を占めるテキストの一つである。『禮記』第四十二篇として収録されていたが、南宋の朱熹（しゅき）によって『論語』『孟子』『中庸』と共に「四書」として編集され、程朱理學の根本経典として確立された。

「大学」の語義については、注釈家によって多様な解釈が提出されてきた。鄭玄（ていげん）は「広やかに学ぶ」と釈し、朱熹は「大人之学」（大人としての学）と解釈し、修身を根本として齐家・治国・平天下に至る道を指すとした。

本文は「三纲领」（さんこうりゅう）と「八条目」（はちじょうもく）という二重構造によって構成される。「三纲领」とは、明明徳（めいめいとく）・親民（しんみん）・止於至善（しконец善に止まる）を指し、それぞれ人固有の徳性を発明すること、德をもって民を導くこと、そして最高善に到達することを意味している。

「八条目」は格物・致知・誠意・正心・修身・斉家・治国・平天下からなり、内面的修養から外面的治世へと段階的に進む実践的プログラムを提示している。特に「格物致知」（かくぶつちち）については、朱熹が「即物窮理」（そつぶつきゅうり）の立場から、外物の研究を通じて天理を認識すると解釈したのに対し、明代の王陽明（おうようめい）は「致知在格物」（ちちざいがくぶつ）という解釈を提唱し、心の善性を直接伸ばすことのほうを強調した。

『大學』の政治哲学的核心は、「修身為本」（しゅうしんいをほんとす）という原則にある。天子から庶民に至るまで、一切の者が修身を根本とすべきであり、個人の道徳的完成が家庭的調和・国家的秩序・天下的太平の前提となるという構造は、儒教的政治観の中核を成している。
